# Classic Chicken Salad

> A no-cook recipe that turns a Costco rotisserie chicken into a batch of creamy, crunchy chicken salad. Perfect on sandwiches, crackers, or lettuce wraps.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Rotisserie Chicken — 1 chicken (about 3 cups shredded) (~$4.99, 3 lb whole chicken)
- Kirkland Signature Pecan Halves — 1/2 cup, roughly chopped (~$13.99, 2 lb bag)
- Red Seedless Grapes — 1 cup, halved (~$6.99, 3 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Mayonnaise — 1/2 cup (~$6.49, 64 oz jar)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 stalks Celery, diced
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon Lemon juice
- 1/2 teaspoon Salt
- 1/4 teaspoon Black pepper
- Bread, crackers, or lettuce wraps *(for serving)*

## Instructions
1. Shred the rotisserie chicken into bite-sized pieces, discarding skin and bones. You want about 3 cups.
2. In a large bowl, combine the mayo, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Stir until smooth.
3. Add the shredded chicken, diced celery, halved grapes, and chopped pecans to the bowl.
4. Fold everything together gently until the chicken is evenly coated. Don't overmix — you want chunks, not paste.
5. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed.
6. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to let the flavors come together.
7. Serve on bread, with crackers, or scooped into lettuce cups.

> **Tip:** Make this right when you get home from Costco while the chicken is still warm — it absorbs the dressing better and shreds more easily.

## Storage
Refrigerate in an airtight container up to 4 days. Does not freeze well due to the mayo and grapes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Stuff into a halved avocado for a low-carb lunch
- Scoop onto Costco croissants for chicken salad sandwiches
- Spread into a tortilla wrap with lettuce and tomato
