# Overnight Breakfast Casserole

> A hearty, make-ahead breakfast bake loaded with bacon, eggs, hash browns, and cheese. Assemble it the night before and just pop it in the oven in the morning.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 50 min | **Servings:** 12 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 12 eggs (~$1.80, 5-dozen pack at ~$8.99)
- Kirkland Signature Bacon — 1 lb (~$5.50, 4-pack at ~$21.99)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Sharp Cheddar — 3 cups (~$3.00, 2.5 lb bag at ~$11.99)
- Kirkland Signature Frozen Hash Browns — 1 bag, 30 oz (~$2.50, 8 lb bag at ~$6.99)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 cup whole milk
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp smoked paprika (optional)
- Cooking spray or butter for greasing
- Optional: diced green bell pepper, sliced green onions

## Instructions
1. Cook the bacon in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy, about 8-10 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate and let it cool, then crumble or chop into bite-sized pieces.
2. Grease a 9x13 baking dish with cooking spray or butter.
3. Spread the frozen hash browns in an even layer across the bottom of the dish. No need to thaw them first.
4. Scatter the crumbled bacon evenly over the hash browns. If you're adding bell pepper or green onions, add them here.
5. Sprinkle 2 cups of the shredded cheddar over the bacon layer.
6. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, and smoked paprika until smooth and well combined.
7. Pour the egg mixture evenly over everything in the dish. Use a fork to gently press down so the hash browns absorb some of the liquid.
8. Top with the remaining 1 cup of cheddar cheese.
9. Cover tightly with plastic wrap or foil and refrigerate overnight, or for at least 4 hours.
10. When you're ready to bake, preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Remove the casserole from the fridge and let it sit on the counter while the oven heats up.
11. Bake covered with foil for 35 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 15 minutes until the eggs are set in the center and the cheese on top is golden brown.
12. Let it rest for 10 minutes before cutting. This helps it hold together when you slice it.

> **Tip:** Test doneness by gently shaking the dish -- the center shouldn't jiggle. If the top is browning too fast but the center is still soft, cover it back up with foil and give it another 10 minutes. Every oven is different.

## Storage
Keeps in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat individual portions in the microwave for 1-2 minutes. Freezes well for up to 2 months -- cut into portions, wrap in plastic wrap, then foil. Reheat from frozen at 350°F for 20-25 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Cut a square and stuff it into a warm tortilla with salsa for a breakfast burrito
- Crumble a leftover slice over toast and top with hot sauce
- Chop it up and fry it in a skillet with extra eggs for a hash-style scramble
