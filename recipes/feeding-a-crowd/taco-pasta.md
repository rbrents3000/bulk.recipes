# Taco Pasta

> What happens when Taco Tuesday meets pasta night, and somehow everyone wins.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Servings:** 10 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef — 2 lbs (~$22.99, 4-pack of 1 lb rolls, use 2)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Mexican Blend Cheese — 2 cups (~$13.99, 2.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Salsa — 1.5 cups (~$8.99, 2-pack jars)
- Kirkland Signature Sour Cream — for topping (~$4.49, 3 lb tub)
- Rotini Pasta — 1 lb box (~$1.99, or use whatever short pasta you have)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 packet (or 3 tablespoons) Taco seasoning
- 1 cup Water
- Salt to taste
- Optional toppings: sliced jalapeños, diced tomatoes, chopped cilantro, sliced black olives

## Instructions
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the rotini according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
2. In the same pot (one pot = one hero), brown the ground beef over medium-high heat, breaking it into crumbles. Drain any excess fat.
3. Add the taco seasoning and 1 cup of water. Stir and let it simmer for 2–3 minutes until the sauce thickens.
4. Add the salsa and stir to combine. Let it bubble for another minute.
5. Add the cooked pasta back to the pot and toss everything together until the noodles are coated in beefy, taco-y goodness.
6. Sprinkle the shredded cheese over the top. Cover with a lid for 2 minutes to let it get melty and irresistible.
7. Serve straight from the pot (because you're feeding a crowd and presentation is a suggestion, not a rule). Top with sour cream and whatever else you've got.

> **Tip:** This recipe scales absurdly well. Hosting 20 people? Double it. The Costco-sized cheese bag and salsa jars were literally designed for this moment. Also, swap ground beef for ground turkey if you want, but don't tell the taco purists.

## Storage
Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The pasta soaks up the sauce overnight and honestly gets even better. Reheat on the stove with a splash of water or in the microwave. Freezes well for up to 2 months.

## Leftover Ideas
- Stuff into bell peppers and bake at 375°F for 20 minutes for taco-stuffed peppers
- Scoop onto tortilla chips with extra cheese for the world's best nachos
- Roll into flour tortillas with sour cream for taco pasta burritos (don't knock it)
