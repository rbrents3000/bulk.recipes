# No-Bake Granola Bars

> Four ingredients, zero oven time, and they taste better than anything in a wrapper.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 0 min (2 hr chill) | **Servings:** 16 bars | **Cost:** ~$0.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Rolled Oats — 3 cups (~$8.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Peanut Butter — 1 cup (~$10.99, 2-pack jars)
- Kirkland Chocolate Chips — 1/2 cup (~$10.99, 4.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Honey — 1/2 cup (~$12.99, 5 lb jug)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt

## Instructions
1. Line a 9x13 baking pan with parchment paper.
2. In a large microwave-safe bowl, combine peanut butter and honey. Microwave for 30-45 seconds until warm and easily stirrable.
3. Add vanilla and salt. Stir to combine.
4. Fold in rolled oats until fully coated. Then fold in chocolate chips.
5. Press the mixture firmly and evenly into the prepared pan. Use the bottom of a measuring cup to really pack it down -- this is what keeps the bars from crumbling.
6. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours until firm.
7. Lift out using the parchment paper and cut into 16 bars.

> **Tip:** Pressing the mixture down firmly is the secret. Loose granola bars become loose granola. Commit to the press.

## Storage
Keep refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 1 week. They can also be frozen individually wrapped for up to 2 months. Let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes before eating.

## Leftover Ideas
- Crumble over yogurt or ice cream as a topping
- Pulse in a food processor to make a pie crust base
- Melt a bar into warm oatmeal for a peanut butter chocolate breakfast
