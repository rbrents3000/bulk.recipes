# Hummus & Veggie Cups

> The healthy snack that's actually good enough to eat on purpose.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$1.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Organic Hummus — 1 tub (~$7.99, 2-pack)
- Bell Peppers, mixed colors — 3 peppers (~$5.99, 6-pack)
- Organic Baby Carrots — about half the bag (~$4.99, 3 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large cucumber
- 1 pint cherry tomatoes
- Extra virgin olive oil for drizzling
- Smoked paprika or everything bagel seasoning for topping
- Pita chips or crackers (optional, for when the veggies alone aren't cutting it)

## Instructions
1. Wash all your vegetables. Yes, even the ones that say "pre-washed." Trust issues are healthy sometimes.
2. Slice the bell peppers in half, remove seeds and membranes, then cut into strips about the width of your finger.
3. Cut the cucumber in half lengthwise, then into quarters, then into 3-inch sticks.
4. Leave cherry tomatoes whole for easy dipping, or halve them if you prefer.
5. Trim the baby carrots if any look particularly chunky. Most are fine as-is.
6. Spoon about 3 tablespoons of hummus into each small cup, ramekin, or the bottom of a wide-mouth mason jar.
7. Drizzle a little olive oil over each portion of hummus and dust with smoked paprika or everything bagel seasoning. This takes 10 seconds and makes it look like you have your life together.
8. Arrange veggie sticks around the hummus for easy dipping.
9. If you're meal prepping, pack veggies on top of the hummus in the jar — they stay above the hummus and don't get soggy.
10. Toss in a handful of pita chips or crackers on the side for variety.
11. For a fancier presentation, arrange everything on a big platter with the hummus bowl in the center. Add some olives, pickled peppers, or feta chunks if you want to go full mezze.

> **Tip:** For meal prep, wide-mouth mason jars are the move. Hummus on the bottom, veggies standing upright on top. They keep for 4-5 days and grab right out of the fridge for lunches or snack time.

> **Tip:** The Costco 6-pack of bell peppers and 3 lb bag of carrots will give you way more than one batch. Prep the extra veggies at the same time and store them in water in the fridge — they'll stay crisp for days.

## Variations
- **Spicy:** Stir sriracha or harissa into the hummus before portioning. A little goes a long way.
- **Herby:** Mix in chopped fresh dill, parsley, or za'atar seasoning for a Mediterranean vibe.
- **Loaded:** Top each hummus cup with diced cucumber, tomato, a drizzle of tahini, and a sprinkle of sumac.

## Storage
Prepped veggie sticks keep up to 5 days in the fridge. Store carrots and cucumber submerged in water in sealed containers for maximum crunch. Peppers do fine in a zip-lock bag with a damp paper towel. Hummus keeps about 7 days after opening. Always store the veggies and hummus separately until you're ready to eat.

## Leftover Ideas
- Thin leftover hummus with a splash of warm water and toss with pasta for a surprisingly creamy sauce
- Spread hummus on sandwiches or wraps as a mayo alternative
- Toss extra veggies into a stir-fry, fried rice, or sheet pan dinner
- Blend hummus with lemon juice and olive oil for a quick salad dressing
- Stuff extra peppers with cream cheese for a different snack entirely
- Use hummus as a pizza base instead of tomato sauce — surprisingly great with roasted veggies on top
