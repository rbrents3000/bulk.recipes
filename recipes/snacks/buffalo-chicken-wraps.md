# Buffalo Chicken Wraps

> Rotisserie chicken's spicy second act. Takes five minutes if you don't count the time spent wondering why you don't make these every day.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Rotisserie Chicken — about half, shredded (~$4.99)
- Kirkland Flour Tortillas — 6 burrito-size (~$4.49, 10-pack)
- Kirkland Ranch Dressing — a few tablespoons (~$6.99)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/3 cup Frank's RedHot or your favorite buffalo sauce
- 2 cups shredded iceberg or romaine lettuce
- 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese
- 2 stalks celery, diced small (optional, for crunch)
- 1 avocado, sliced (optional, but strongly encouraged)

## Instructions
1. Pull about half the meat off the rotisserie chicken. Use two forks and go with the grain — dark meat from the thighs shreds easiest.
2. Place the shredded chicken in a medium bowl and toss with the buffalo sauce until everything is evenly coated. Start with 1/4 cup and add more if you like it hot.
3. Warm the tortillas: stack them between two damp paper towels and microwave for 20-30 seconds. This makes them pliable and less likely to crack.
4. Lay out a tortilla on a clean surface. Think of it as a blank canvas for spicy art.
5. Lay down a line of shredded lettuce across the center, leaving about 2 inches on each side.
6. Add a generous portion of the buffalo chicken on top of the lettuce.
7. Sprinkle with cheese, add diced celery if using, and lay a few avocado slices across.
8. Drizzle with ranch dressing. Be generous — the ranch is doing important cooling work here.
9. Fold in both sides of the tortilla, then roll tightly from the bottom up, tucking the filling in as you go.
10. Slice in half on a diagonal if you want it to look restaurant-worthy.
11. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
12. For a crispy finish, heat a dry skillet over medium heat and place each wrap seam-side down for 1-2 minutes. Flip and sear the other side until golden.
13. Slice each wrap in half on a diagonal. It looks better and is easier to eat. Win-win.

> **Tip:** For crispier wraps, sear them seam-side down in a dry skillet over medium heat for 1-2 minutes after rolling. Flip and do the other side. The outside gets golden and toasty while the inside stays saucy. This one step takes them from good to "I need to make these again tomorrow."

> **Tip:** If you like blue cheese more than ranch, nobody's stopping you. Crumbled blue cheese inside the wrap with a blue cheese dressing drizzle is the classic wing combo in wrap form.

> **Tip:** Save the other half of the rotisserie chicken for tomorrow. It'll keep in the fridge for 3-4 days, giving you a head start on chicken salad, quesadillas, or another round of these wraps.

## Variations
- **Honey Buffalo:** Mix 2 tbsp honey into the buffalo sauce for a sweet-heat version.
- **Greek Buffalo:** Swap ranch for tzatziki and add diced cucumber and red onion.
- **BBQ Chicken Wrap:** Swap buffalo sauce for BBQ sauce and add crispy onion strings.

## Storage
Wrap each one tightly in foil and store in the fridge for 2-3 days. Full honesty: these are best eaten the day you make them. The lettuce starts losing its crunch after a day. If meal prepping, keep the lettuce and avocado separate and add them fresh when you're ready to eat.

## Leftover Ideas
- Chop up a leftover wrap and toss it over greens for a buffalo chicken salad
- Unwrap the filling and stuff it into a quesadilla with extra cheese
- Pile the buffalo chicken on top of nachos with ranch and pickled jalapeños
- Skip the tortilla and serve the buffalo chicken over rice for a quick bowl
- Toss leftover buffalo chicken into mac and cheese for a wildly good comfort food mashup
