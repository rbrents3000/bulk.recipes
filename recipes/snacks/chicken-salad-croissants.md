# Chicken Salad Croissants

> The lunch that makes coworkers jealous and earns you unsolicited recipe requests.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Rotisserie Chicken — 1, shredded (~$4.99, whole chicken)
- Costco Butter Croissants — 6 (~$5.99, 12-pack)
- Costco Red Grapes — 1 cup, halved (~$6.99, 3 lb bag)
- Kirkland Pecans — 1/2 cup, roughly chopped (~$12.99, 2 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 2 stalks celery, finely diced
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Lettuce leaves (optional)

## Instructions
1. Shred the rotisserie chicken into a large bowl. You should get about 3-4 cups of meat.
2. Add mayo, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, celery, halved grapes, and chopped pecans. Stir until everything is coated.
3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
4. Slice croissants in half horizontally.
5. Add a lettuce leaf if using, then pile chicken salad generously onto each croissant.
6. Accept compliments graciously.

> **Tip:** The rotisserie chicken is already seasoned, so taste your salad before adding salt. Between the chicken, mustard, and mayo, you may not need much.

## Storage
Keep chicken salad (without croissants) refrigerated for up to 3 days. Assemble sandwiches just before eating to keep croissants from getting soggy.

## Leftover Ideas
- Serve the chicken salad on crackers for an easy appetizer
- Stuff into lettuce wraps for a low-carb version
- Scoop over a bed of mixed greens for a chicken salad salad
