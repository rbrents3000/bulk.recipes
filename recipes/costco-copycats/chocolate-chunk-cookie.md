# Copycat Costco Double Chocolate Chunk Cookie

> The food court cookie that quietly became everyone's favorite impulse buy. $2.49 there. About fifty cents here. Do the math.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 12 min | **Servings:** 12 cookies | **Cost:** ~$0.50/cookie

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips — 2 cups (~$9.99, 4.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 1 cup / 2 sticks (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Cage-Free Eggs — 2 large (~$7.99, 5 dozen)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

## Instructions
1. Brown the butter: melt both sticks in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, swirling occasionally. It will foam, then turn golden with nutty brown flecks on the bottom — that's when you pull it. Pour into a large bowl and let cool for 5 minutes.
2. Add brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla to the browned butter. Whisk until smooth and slightly thickened.
3. Add eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. The mixture should look glossy.
4. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
5. Fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture with a spatula — stir just until combined, no more.
6. Fold in the chocolate chips. The dough will be thick and rich.
7. Scoop into large balls, about 3 ounces each. Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet with 3 inches between them.
8. Bake at 350°F for 10-12 minutes. Pull them when the centers still look slightly underdone — they'll firm up as they cool.

> **Tip:** Browning the butter is what makes these taste like the food court version. It adds nutty, toffee-like depth that regular melted butter simply cannot match. Don't skip this step.

## Storage
Keep in an airtight container for up to 5 days. Freeze unbaked dough balls on a sheet pan, then transfer to a bag — bake from frozen at 350°F for 13-15 minutes for fresh cookies anytime.

## Leftover Ideas
- Sandwich vanilla ice cream between two cookies for ice cream sandwiches
- Crumble over a bowl of ice cream for the world's best topping
- Warm a cookie in the microwave for 10 seconds and pretend it just came out of the oven
