# Copycat Costco Mini Quiche Tray

> The Costco deli sells a tray of 24 mini quiches in lorraine and florentine flavors. Make your own for half the price and serve them hot instead of room-temperature-mystery-warm.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 24 mini quiches | **Cost:** ~$0.75/each

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Thick-Sliced Bacon — 6 slices (~$18.99, 4 x 1 lb packs)
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 6 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Swiss or Gruyere — 1 cup (~$12.99, 2 lb pack)
- Kirkland Signature Heavy Cream — 3/4 cup (~$5.99, 2-quart carton)

## Other Ingredients
**For all quiches:**
- 2 packages (15 oz each) refrigerated pie crust (or 24 mini tart shells)
- 6 eggs
- 3/4 cup heavy cream
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Lorraine filling (half the batch):**
- 6 slices bacon, cooked and crumbled
- 1/2 cup shredded Swiss/Gruyere

**Florentine filling (half the batch):**
- 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed very dry
- 1/2 cup shredded Swiss/Gruyere
- 2 tablespoons sun-dried tomatoes, chopped *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 24-cup mini muffin tin (or work in two batches of 12).
2. Roll out pie crust and cut into 24 rounds using a 3-inch cutter. Press each round into a muffin cup.
3. Whisk together eggs, heavy cream, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
4. **Lorraine cups:** Place bacon crumbles and cheese in 12 of the crusts. Pour egg mixture to fill 3/4 full.
5. **Florentine cups:** Place spinach, cheese, and sun-dried tomatoes in the other 12 crusts. Pour egg mixture to fill 3/4 full.
6. Bake 22-25 minutes until the filling is puffed and set and the crust is golden.
7. Let cool 5 minutes in the tin, then pop them out.

> **Tip:** Don't overfill — the egg mixture puffs up. Fill to 3/4 and you'll get perfect little domes.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 3 days. Reheat in a 350°F oven for 8-10 minutes. Freeze for up to 2 months — reheat from frozen at 375°F for 12 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pack 3-4 in a lunch box with a small salad for a complete meal
- Serve at room temperature as part of a brunch spread
