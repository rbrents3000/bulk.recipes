# Triple Chocolate Loaf

> Three kinds of chocolate in one loaf. The bakery's 2025 MVP, now in your kitchen.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 55 min | **Servings:** 10 slices | **Cost:** ~$0.75/slice

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips — 1.5 cups (~$9.99, 4.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 1/2 cup (1 stick) (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Cage-Free Eggs — 3 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
- 3/4 cup sugar
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1/2 cup white chocolate chips *(for the drizzle on top)*

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a standard 9x5 loaf pan and line with parchment paper.
2. Melt butter and 1 cup of the chocolate chips together in a saucepan over low heat, stirring constantly. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
3. In a large bowl, whisk eggs, sugar, vanilla, and sour cream until smooth.
4. Pour the melted chocolate mixture into the egg mixture and fold together.
5. Sift flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt over the batter. Fold in gently — you want it just combined, not beaten into submission.
6. Fold in the remaining 1/2 cup chocolate chips.
7. Pour into prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
8. Bake 50-55 minutes until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with moist crumbs (not wet batter).
9. Cool in pan 15 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely.
10. Melt white chocolate chips and drizzle over the top in dramatic zigzags. This is the triple in triple chocolate.

> **Tip:** Let this cool COMPLETELY before slicing. I know it's hard. The texture sets up as it cools and goes from "brownie in a loaf pan" to "actual chocolate loaf."

## Storage
Keeps 5 days wrapped tightly at room temperature. Freezes beautifully — wrap individual slices for grab-and-go chocolate emergencies.

## Leftover Ideas
- French toast made with thick chocolate loaf slices (yes, really)
- Chocolate bread pudding with a splash of bourbon cream
- Crumble over vanilla ice cream for an instant sundae
