# Copycat Costco France Quiche Lorraine

> A big, custardy quiche loaded with bacon and cheese — Costco France sells a family-sized version in the deli. This one feeds 8, uses Kirkland bacon and eggs, and is the ultimate brunch centerpiece.

*Costco France sells this as a family-size deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Thick-Sliced Bacon — 8 slices (~$18.99, 4 x 1 lb packs)
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 5 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Kirkland Signature Heavy Cream — 1 cup (~$5.99, 2-quart carton)
- Gruyere or Swiss Cheese — 1.5 cups shredded (~$12.99, 2 lb pack)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 deep-dish pie crust (9-inch, store-bought or homemade)
- 1/2 cup whole milk
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
- 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Place pie crust in a 9-inch deep-dish pie plate. Prick the bottom with a fork. Blind bake with pie weights or dried beans for 10 minutes. Remove weights and bake 5 more minutes until lightly golden.
2. Cook bacon until crispy. Crumble into pieces.
3. Whisk together eggs, heavy cream, milk, nutmeg, salt, and pepper until smooth.
4. Scatter bacon and cheese across the bottom of the par-baked crust.
5. Pour egg mixture over the top. It should come nearly to the top of the crust.
6. Bake 35-40 minutes until the center is just set — it should jiggle slightly like Jell-O when you tap the pan. It will firm up as it cools.
7. Let rest 10 minutes before slicing. Sprinkle with chives.

> **Tip:** Don't skip the blind bake — without it, the bottom crust is a soggy mess. The 15 minutes upfront saves the whole quiche.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat slices in a 325°F oven for 10 minutes. Freezes well for up to 2 months — thaw overnight, then reheat.

## Leftover Ideas
- Eat cold with a side salad — quiche is one of the few things that's great at any temperature
- Cut into small squares and serve as appetizer bites
