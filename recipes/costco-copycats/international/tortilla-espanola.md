# Copycat Costco Spain Tortilla Española

> The iconic Spanish potato omelette — golden, custardy, and dead simple. Five ingredients, zero fuss, and it somehow tastes better at room temperature. Costco Spain sells family-sized wedges in the deli, and honestly, the homemade version is even better.

*Costco Spain sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 30 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 10 eggs (~$0.80, from 5-dozen pack)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 1 cup (~$1.50, 2L bottle)
- Russet or Gold Potatoes — 2 lbs (~$1.50, 10 lb bag)
- Yellow Onion — 1 large (~$0.30, 5 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- Freshly cracked black pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Peel and slice potatoes into thin rounds (about 1/8 inch thick). Halve the onion and slice into thin half-moons. Season everything generously with salt.
2. Heat olive oil in a 10-inch nonstick skillet over medium heat — you want enough oil to nearly submerge the potatoes. Add potatoes and onion, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook for 18-20 minutes, stirring gently every few minutes. You're not frying here — you're gently poaching. The potatoes should be completely tender but not browned.
3. Crack eggs into a large bowl and beat well. Strain the potatoes and onion from the oil (save that liquid gold for future cooking). Add the warm potato-onion mixture to the eggs and stir gently. Let it sit for 5 minutes so the potatoes absorb the egg.
4. Heat 2 tablespoons of the reserved olive oil in the same skillet over medium heat. Pour in the egg-potato mixture, spreading it evenly. Cook undisturbed for 4-5 minutes until the edges are set and the bottom is golden.
5. **The flip:** Place a large flat plate over the skillet. In one confident motion, flip the tortilla onto the plate. Slide it back into the pan, uncooked side down. Cook another 3-4 minutes until just set — the center should still be slightly custardy.
6. Slide onto a serving plate and let it rest at least 10 minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature, cut into wedges.

> **Tip:** Don't overthink the flip. Commit to it. Hesitation is how tortillas end up on the floor. And don't overcook the center — a slightly custardy interior is the mark of a proper tortilla.

## Storage
Tortilla Española actually improves overnight. Keep it covered at room temperature for up to 24 hours, or refrigerate for up to 4 days. Serve cold or at room temperature — reheating is optional but not traditional. Wrap individual wedges tightly and freeze for up to 2 months.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice and stuff into a crusty baguette for a bocadillo de tortilla
- Dice into cubes and serve as pintxos with toothpicks
- Serve alongside a simple green salad for an effortless lunch
