# Copycat Costco Canada Tourtière

> A Québécois meat pie with seasoned ground pork and beef under a flaky double crust — Costco Canada sells this in the deli every holiday season. Make one yourself and serve it on Christmas Eve like a proper Canadien.

*Costco Canada sells this as a seasonal deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 50 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef 88/12 — 1 lb (~$6.99/lb)
- Kirkland Signature Ground Pork — 1 lb (~$3.99/lb, 4-pack)
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 1 egg for wash (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Russet Potatoes — 1 large, peeled and diced small (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 refrigerated pie crusts (for top and bottom)
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup beef broth
- 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon cloves (ground)
- 1/4 teaspoon allspice
- 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon water (egg wash)

## Instructions
1. Cook ground pork and beef in a large skillet over medium heat, breaking into small pieces.
2. Add onion and cook 4-5 minutes until softened. Add garlic, cinnamon, cloves, allspice, thyme, and bay leaf. Cook 1 minute.
3. Add diced potato and beef broth. Simmer 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until potatoes are tender and most liquid is absorbed. The filling should be moist but not soupy. Discard bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper. Let cool.
4. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
5. Press one pie crust into a 9-inch pie plate. Fill with the cooled meat mixture. Top with the second crust. Crimp edges to seal and cut a few small slits in the top for steam.
6. Brush with egg wash.
7. Bake 35-40 minutes until the crust is deep golden brown.
8. Let rest 10 minutes before slicing.

> **Tip:** The warm spices (cinnamon, cloves, allspice) are subtle but essential — they're what make this a tourtière instead of just a meat pie.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freezes unbaked for up to 3 months — bake from frozen, adding 15-20 minutes and covering with foil for the first 30 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Serve a cold slice with ketchup — that's actually how a lot of Québécois eat it
- Crumble leftover filling into a soup or stew
