# Copycat Costco Japan Chicken Karaage

> Japanese fried chicken — marinated in soy, ginger, and garlic, coated in potato starch, and fried until shatteringly crispy. Costco Japan sells this in their deli and it flies off the shelf. Now it flies off your plate.

*Costco Japan sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 15 min + 30 min marinate | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs — 2 lbs (~$8.00, 6.5 lb pack)
- Kirkland Signature Sesame Oil — 1 tablespoon (~$6.99, 20 oz)

## Other Ingredients
**Marinade:**
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sake (or dry white wine)
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 3 cloves garlic, grated
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon sugar

**Coating:**
- 3/4 cup potato starch (or cornstarch)
- Vegetable oil for frying (about 3 cups)

**Serving:**
- Lemon wedges
- Japanese mayo (Kewpie) *(optional)*
- Shredded cabbage

## Instructions
1. Cut chicken thighs into large bite-sized pieces (about 1.5 inches).
2. Combine all marinade ingredients in a bowl. Add chicken and toss to coat. Marinate at least 30 minutes (up to overnight in the fridge).
3. Heat oil to 340°F (170°C) in a deep pot.
4. Remove chicken from marinade and toss in potato starch until evenly coated. Shake off excess.
5. Fry in batches for 4-5 minutes until golden brown and cooked through. Don't crowd the pot.
6. Drain on a wire rack. Let rest 2 minutes.
7. Serve over shredded cabbage with lemon wedges and Kewpie mayo.

> **Tip:** Potato starch gives a lighter, crunchier crust than flour. It's the traditional choice and you can find it at most grocery stores in the baking aisle.

## Storage
Best eaten fresh. Reheat in a 425°F oven for 8-10 minutes to re-crisp. Don't microwave.

## Leftover Ideas
- Toss with rice, furikake, and Kewpie mayo for a karaage rice bowl
- Chop and tuck into a sandwich with slaw
- Serve cold in a bento box with rice and pickled vegetables
