# Copycat Costco Korea Japchae (Glass Noodle Stir-Fry)

> Chewy sweet potato glass noodles stir-fried with tender beef, spinach, carrots, and mushrooms in a sweet soy-sesame sauce. Costco Korea sells this as a ready-to-eat side — and honestly, this homemade version is better.

*Costco Korea sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Beef Sirloin — 8 oz, thinly sliced (~$8.99/lb)
- Kirkland Signature Sesame Oil — 2 tablespoons (~$6.99, 20 oz)
- Kirkland Signature Baby Spinach — 6 oz (~$4.99, 1 lb clamshell)
- Whole Carrots — 2 medium (~$4.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Sliced Mushrooms — 8 oz (~$4.49, 24 oz pack)

## Other Ingredients
**Noodles:**
- 8 oz sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon) — find these at any Asian grocery, or order online

**Sauce:**
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

**Everything else:**
- 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- 2 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

## Instructions
1. Cook dangmyeon noodles according to package directions (usually 6-7 minutes in boiling water). Drain, rinse with cold water, and snip a few times with scissors so they're not one giant tangle. Toss with 1 tablespoon sesame oil.
2. **Sauce:** Whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, minced garlic, remaining 1 tablespoon sesame oil, and black pepper. Set aside.
3. **Beef:** Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Stir-fry beef slices for 2 minutes until just browned. Remove and set aside.
4. **Vegetables:** In the same pan, stir-fry onion and carrots (julienned into matchsticks) for 2 minutes. Add mushrooms and cook another 2 minutes. Add spinach and green onions, cook just until spinach wilts — about 30 seconds.
5. Add noodles and beef back to the pan. Pour the sauce over everything. Toss with tongs for 1-2 minutes until the sauce coats every strand and the noodles turn glossy.
6. Garnish with sesame seeds. Serve warm, at room temperature, or cold — japchae is one of those rare dishes that's great at any temperature.

> **Tip:** Dangmyeon noodles are the non-negotiable here — regular glass noodles (mung bean) won't give you the same bouncy, chewy texture. The sweet potato starch is what makes japchae feel like japchae.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days — japchae actually gets more flavorful as it sits. The noodles may stick together; just microwave for 30 seconds or toss with a splash of sesame oil before serving. Freezes well for up to 1 month.

## Leftover Ideas
- Stuff into a kimbap roll with rice and pickled radish
- Top with a fried egg for a quick noodle bowl
- Wrap in lettuce leaves for a low-carb snack
