# Copycat Costco Korea Galbi (Marinated Short Ribs)

> Costco Korea's single most iconic prepared item — sweet, savory, deeply caramelized beef short ribs marinated in soy, Korean pear, garlic, and sesame. Once you nail this marinade, you'll never look at plain grilled beef the same way again.

*Costco Korea sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 20 min + overnight marinade | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$5.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Costco Beef Short Ribs, flanken-cut (cross-cut) — 4 lbs (~$28.00)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Soy Sauce — 1/2 cup (~$8.99, 40 oz)
- Kirkland Signature Sesame Oil — 3 tablespoons (~$6.99, 20 oz)
- Fresh garlic — 10 cloves (~$4.99, 3 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
**Marinade:**
- 1 large Korean pear (or 2 ripe Bosc pears), grated
- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons mirin
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 1 medium onion, grated
- 3 green onions, sliced

**Garnish:**
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Sliced green onions

## Instructions
1. **Marinade:** Blend or finely grate the Korean pear. Combine with soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, mirin, rice vinegar, grated onion, minced garlic, and black pepper. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
2. Place flanken-cut short ribs in a large zip-top bag or shallow container. Pour marinade over the ribs, making sure every piece is coated. Seal and refrigerate overnight (at least 8 hours, up to 24 hours). The pear is doing double duty here — tenderizing and sweetening.
3. Remove ribs from the fridge 30 minutes before cooking to take the chill off.
4. **Grill method:** Heat grill to high (around 500°F). Grill ribs 2-3 minutes per side until you get deep, sticky char marks. The sugar in the marinade caramelizes fast, so watch them closely.
5. **Pan method:** Heat a cast-iron skillet or heavy pan over high heat until smoking. Cook ribs in a single layer (work in batches — don't crowd) for 2-3 minutes per side.
6. Transfer to a platter, garnish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately with steamed rice and kimchi.

> **Tip:** Flanken-cut (cross-cut) short ribs are the move here — they're sliced thin across the bone, so you get multiple little bone cross-sections in each strip. This is what gives galbi its signature look and fast cook time. If your Costco only has English-cut (thick, single-bone) short ribs, ask the butcher to cut them flanken-style, or slice them yourself with a sharp knife.

## Storage
Cooked galbi keeps in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in a hot skillet to restore some of that caramelized crust — the microwave will work in a pinch but you'll lose the texture. Uncooked marinated ribs freeze beautifully for up to 2 months — thaw overnight in the fridge before grilling.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice thin and pile into rice bowls with pickled veggies
- Chop and fold into fried rice with a fried egg on top
- Stuff into lettuce wraps with gochujang mayo
- Dice and toss into japchae (glass noodle stir-fry)
