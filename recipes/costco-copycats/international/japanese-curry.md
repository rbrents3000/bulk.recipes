# Copycat Costco Japan Japanese Curry

> Rich, thick, and mildly sweet — Japanese curry is nothing like Indian curry and everything like a warm hug. Costco Japan sells it as a ready-to-eat meal. Make a big pot with Costco chicken and rice and feed the whole family.

*Costco Japan sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 35 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs — 1.5 lbs (~$8.00, 6.5 lb pack)
- Kirkland Signature Jasmine Rice — 3 cups dry (~$16.99, 25 lb bag)
- Russet Potatoes — 2 large, cubed (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 box (8 serving size) Japanese curry roux (S&B Golden Curry or Vermont Curry — medium or hot)
- 2 carrots, cut into large chunks
- 1 large onion, diced
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 4 cups water
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon honey *(optional — for extra sweetness)*
- Fukujinzuke (pickled relish) for serving *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Cut chicken into bite-sized pieces. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Brown chicken pieces for 3-4 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, cook onion 5 minutes until softened and starting to caramelize. Add carrots and potatoes, stir for 2 minutes.
4. Add water, soy sauce, and chicken. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook 20 minutes until potatoes and carrots are tender.
5. Turn off heat. Break curry roux into pieces and stir into the pot until fully dissolved. Add honey if using.
6. Return to low heat and simmer 5 more minutes, stirring often, until sauce is thick and glossy.
7. Serve over rice with fukujinzuke on the side.

> **Tip:** Let the pot cool slightly before adding the roux — if the liquid is at a full boil, the roux can clump instead of dissolving smoothly.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat with a splash of water — the curry thickens considerably in the fridge. Remove potatoes before freezing (they get mealy) — everything else freezes great for up to 2 months.

## Leftover Ideas
- Spoon over a crispy tonkatsu cutlet for katsu curry
- Use as filling for Japanese curry bread (pan-fried buns)
- Pour over udon noodles for curry udon
