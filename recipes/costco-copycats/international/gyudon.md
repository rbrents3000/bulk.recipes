# Copycat Costco Japan Gyudon

> A simmered beef bowl that takes 20 minutes and tastes like you ordered it at Yoshinoya. Costco Japan sells gyudon kits in the deli — make it from scratch with thinly sliced Costco beef and you'll never look back.

*Costco Japan sells this as a deli kit — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Beef Ribeye or Sirloin — 1 lb, sliced paper-thin (~$12.99/lb, from meat counter — ask them to slice it thin)
- Kirkland Signature Jasmine Rice — 2 cups dry (~$16.99, 25 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 4 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large onion, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup soy sauce
- 1/4 cup mirin
- 2 tablespoons sake (or dry white wine)
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 cup dashi (or 1 cup water + 1 teaspoon dashi powder)
- 1 inch fresh ginger, sliced thin
- Pickled red ginger (beni shoga) for garnish *(optional)*
- Shichimi togarashi (seven-spice) for garnish *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. In a wide skillet, combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and ginger slices. Bring to a simmer.
3. Add sliced onion. Cook 5 minutes until onion is soft and translucent.
4. Add beef slices in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Simmer 3-4 minutes until just cooked through — don't overcook or the beef gets tough.
5. Crack an egg into each serving bowl of rice (raw or poach it in the simmering broth for 2 minutes for a soft egg). Spoon beef and onion with plenty of broth over the rice.
6. Garnish with pickled ginger and shichimi togarashi.

> **Tip:** Freeze the beef for 30 minutes before slicing — it firms up and you can get paper-thin slices with a sharp knife. Or just ask the Costco meat counter to do it.

## Storage
Refrigerate the beef and sauce up to 3 days. Reheat gently — overcooking makes the beef chewy. Rice stores separately up to 5 days.

## Leftover Ideas
- Top with cheese and broil for a fusion beef bowl
- Roll into a tortilla for a Japanese-style beef wrap
