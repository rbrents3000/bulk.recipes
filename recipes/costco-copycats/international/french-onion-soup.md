# Copycat Costco France French Onion Soup

> Deeply caramelized onions in rich beef broth, topped with a crusty bread cap and a blanket of melted Gruyere. Costco France sells this ready-to-heat — yours will be better because you'll caramelize the onions properly instead of whatever shortcuts they're taking.

*Costco France sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 60 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Yellow Onions — 3 lbs (about 6 large) (~$3.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 4 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Gruyere or Swiss Cheese — 2 cups shredded (~$12.99, 2 lb pack)

## Other Ingredients
- 8 cups beef broth
- 1/2 cup dry white wine (or dry sherry)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 bay leaves
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried)
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 6 thick slices crusty bread (baguette or sourdough)

## Instructions
1. Slice all onions into thin half-moons. This is the boring part. It's worth it.
2. Melt butter with olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onions and sugar, stir to coat. Cook 40-45 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes, until deeply golden brown and jammy. Don't rush this — low and slow is the whole point.
3. Add garlic and flour, stir for 1 minute. Pour in wine and scrape up the browned bits.
4. Add beef broth, bay leaves, and thyme. Simmer 15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Discard bay leaves and thyme stems.
5. Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls. Float a slice of bread on each. Pile shredded Gruyere on top.
6. Broil 3-5 minutes until cheese is bubbly, golden, and draping over the sides of the bowl.

> **Tip:** The onions take 40+ minutes and you will be tempted to crank the heat. Don't. Medium heat, patience, stirring every 5 minutes. This is non-negotiable.

## Storage
Refrigerate the soup (without bread and cheese) up to 5 days. Reheat on the stove, then broil with fresh bread and cheese when serving. Freezes well for up to 3 months.

## Leftover Ideas
- Skip the bread and cheese and use the caramelized onion broth as a gravy base
- Toss the onions into a grilled cheese sandwich
