# Copycat Costco Korea Tteokbokki

> Chewy rice cakes in a sweet-spicy gochujang sauce — Costco Korea sells this as a ready-to-heat kit and it's one of their most popular items. Make it from scratch in 20 minutes.

*Costco Korea sells this as a deli kit — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Sesame Oil — 1 tablespoon (~$6.99, 20 oz)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 lb Korean rice cakes (tteok) — fresh or frozen, from Asian grocery or Costco's Asian aisle
- 2 cups anchovy or dashima broth (or 2 cups water + 1 teaspoon dashi powder)
- 3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
- 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 2 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 2 sheets fish cake (eomuk), cut into triangles *(optional)*
- 1 hard-boiled egg per serving *(optional)*
- Sesame seeds for garnish

## Instructions
1. If using frozen rice cakes, soak in warm water for 10 minutes until softened.
2. Bring broth to a boil in a wide skillet or shallow pot.
3. Stir in gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, and sugar until dissolved.
4. Add rice cakes and fish cake (if using). Simmer 8-10 minutes, stirring frequently, until the rice cakes are soft and the sauce has thickened and coats everything.
5. Add green onions in the last minute. The sauce should be thick and glossy.
6. Serve in bowls topped with sesame seeds, green onions, and a hard-boiled egg.

> **Tip:** Stir often near the end — the starchy sauce thickens fast and can stick to the bottom. If it gets too thick, add a splash of water.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 2 days. The rice cakes will harden in the fridge — reheat in a pan with a splash of water to soften them again. Don't freeze.

## Leftover Ideas
- Add cheese on top and broil for cheesy tteokbokki
- Toss with ramen noodles for a carb-on-carb situation (rabokki)
