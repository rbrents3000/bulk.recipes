# Copycat Costco China Scallion Pancakes

> Crispy, flaky, savory pancakes loaded with green onions — Costco China sells them ready-to-fry and they're addictive. Make your own with flour, oil, and scallions. That's really it.

*Costco China sells these as a prepared deli item — here's how to make them at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 20 min + 30 min rest | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 4 (4 pancakes) | **Cost:** ~$0.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Sesame Oil — 2 tablespoons (~$6.99, 20 oz)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 3/4 cup boiling water
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 6 green onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons sesame oil
- Vegetable oil for pan-frying (about 3 tablespoons per pancake)
- Flaky sea salt for finishing

**Dipping sauce:**
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1/2 teaspoon chili oil or sriracha *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Mix flour and salt in a bowl. Pour in boiling water and stir with chopsticks or a fork until a shaggy dough forms. Knead 5 minutes until smooth. Cover and rest 30 minutes.
2. Divide dough into 4 pieces. Roll each piece into a thin circle (about 10 inches).
3. Brush the surface with sesame oil. Scatter sliced scallions generously across.
4. Roll up tightly into a log, then coil the log into a spiral. Flatten with your palm, then roll out again into a circle (about 7-8 inches). The layers create the flakiness.
5. Heat 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry each pancake 2-3 minutes per side until golden and crispy.
6. Drain on paper towels. Sprinkle with flaky salt. Cut into wedges.
7. Mix dipping sauce ingredients and serve alongside.

> **Tip:** The boiling water makes the dough softer and more pliable than cold water would. Don't substitute — it's the difference between flaky and tough.

## Storage
Best eaten immediately. Cooked pancakes can be re-crisped in a dry skillet. Freeze the rolled-out (uncooked) pancakes between parchment paper for up to 2 months — fry from frozen, adding 1 minute per side.

## Leftover Ideas
- Wrap around leftover meat and vegetables for a savory crepe
- Cut into strips and serve with soup instead of crackers
