# Copycat Costco Spain Croquetas de Jamón

> Crispy, golden ham croquettes with a silky béchamel center loaded with jamón. Costco Spain sells frozen trays of these — now you can make them at home with Costco US ingredients and a little patience.

*Costco Spain sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 30 min + 2 hours chill | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 24 pieces | **Cost:** ~$0.50/piece

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 4 tablespoons (~$5.49, 4-pack 1 lb)
- Kirkland Signature All-Purpose Flour — 1/2 cup (~$5.99, 12 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Whole Milk — 2 cups (~$4.59, 1 gallon)
- Costco Deli Sliced Prosciutto — 6 oz, finely diced (~$11.99, 12 oz pack)

> **Why prosciutto?** Serrano ham (jamón serrano) is the traditional choice, and if your Costco carries it, absolutely use it. But Costco US reliably stocks prosciutto in the deli section, and it gets you 90% of the way there. Nobody is going to complain.

## Other Ingredients
- 1/2 small yellow onion, very finely minced
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- Pinch of nutmeg
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 1 1/2 cups plain breadcrumbs
- Vegetable oil for frying (about 4 cups)

## Instructions
1. Melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook 3-4 minutes until soft and translucent — you don't want any color here.
2. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds. Stir in the flour and cook the roux for 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until it smells biscuity and just barely turns golden.
3. Slowly pour in the milk a splash at a time, whisking like your life depends on it after each addition. You're building a very thick béchamel — think pastry cream, not gravy.
4. Once all the milk is incorporated, cook on low heat for 8-10 minutes, stirring frequently, until the mixture is thick enough to pull away from the sides of the pan. Stir in the diced prosciutto, nutmeg, salt, and white pepper.
5. Spread the mixture onto a parchment-lined baking sheet into an even layer about 3/4 inch thick. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface and refrigerate for **at least 2 hours** (overnight is even better).
6. Once chilled and firm, scoop portions (about 1 tablespoon each) and roll into small logs or ovals with lightly floured hands. Set up a breading station: flour, beaten eggs, breadcrumbs. Dredge each croqueta in flour, dip in egg, then roll in breadcrumbs.
7. Heat oil to 365°F (185°C) in a deep pot or Dutch oven. Fry croquetas in batches of 5-6 for 2-3 minutes, turning once, until deeply golden brown all over. Drain on a wire rack. Serve hot.

> **Tip:** The béchamel needs to be *thick*. If it's too loose, your croquetas will explode in the oil and you'll have a bad time. When in doubt, cook it longer and chill it longer. Patience is the only hard part of this recipe.

> **Tip:** For an even crispier shell, do a double bread: flour, egg, breadcrumbs, then back into egg and breadcrumbs again. Worth the extra step if you're feeling fancy.

## Storage
Unfried croquetas freeze beautifully — arrange on a parchment-lined sheet, freeze solid, then transfer to a zip-top bag. Fry straight from frozen, adding an extra minute to the cook time. Fried croquetas keep in the fridge for 2 days and reheat well in a 400°F oven for 8-10 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Reheat and serve alongside a simple salad for lunch
- Stack into a crusty roll for a croqueta sandwich (bocadillo de croquetas — trust us)
- Serve with tomato soup for dipping
