# Copycat Costco UK Scotch Eggs

> Classic British pub food — soft-boiled eggs wrapped in seasoned sausage meat, coated in crispy panko breadcrumbs, and fried until golden. Costco UK sells these in their deli, and honestly, making them yourself is half the fun.

*Costco UK sells this as a prepared deli item — here's how to make it at home with Costco US ingredients.*

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/egg

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Cage-Free Eggs — 6 eggs (~$8.99, 5 dozen pack)
- Kirkland Signature Breakfast Sausage or Ground Pork — 1.5 lbs (~$10.99, 4 lb pack)
- Kirkland Signature Panko Breadcrumbs — 2 cups (~$5.49, 2.5 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
**Sausage seasoning:**
- 1 teaspoon dried sage
- 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder

**Breading station:**
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 2 eggs, beaten (for egg wash)
- 2 cups panko breadcrumbs

**For frying and serving:**
- Vegetable oil for frying (about 4 cups)
- English mustard or HP sauce

## Instructions
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Gently lower 6 eggs in and boil for exactly 6.5 minutes for a jammy yolk (7 minutes if you want fully set). Transfer to an ice bath immediately and let cool completely. Peel carefully — take your time here, a torn egg means a thin spot in the sausage armor.
2. Mix sausage meat (or ground pork) with sage, thyme, nutmeg, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Divide into 6 equal portions (about 4 oz each).
3. Flatten each sausage portion into a thin patty in your palm. Place a peeled egg in the center and wrap the meat around it, pressing to seal with no gaps. Wet your hands if the meat sticks — this is messy work and that's okay.
4. Set up a breading station: flour in one bowl, beaten eggs in another, panko in a third. Roll each wrapped egg in flour, dip in egg wash, then coat thoroughly in panko. Press the crumbs on firmly — you want full coverage.
5. Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in a deep pot or Dutch oven. Fry eggs in batches of 2-3 for about 5-6 minutes, turning occasionally, until deep golden brown all over. Drain on a wire rack.
6. Let rest 2-3 minutes before cutting in half. Serve with English mustard or HP sauce and try not to eat all six yourself.

> **Tip:** The ice bath is non-negotiable. If your eggs are still warm when you wrap them, the yolks will overcook during frying and you'll lose that gorgeous jammy center. Cold eggs, warm oil — that's the formula.

> **Tip:** If you'd rather skip the frying, you can bake these at 400°F for 25-30 minutes on a wire rack set over a sheet pan. Spray with cooking oil first. They won't be quite as crispy, but they'll still be excellent.

## Storage
Scotch eggs keep in the fridge for up to 3 days and are honestly great cold — that's how the Brits eat them half the time. Reheat in a 375°F oven for 10-12 minutes to re-crisp the coating. They freeze well for up to 1 month — reheat from frozen at 375°F for 18-20 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice in half and serve on top of a salad
- Pack whole for a picnic or lunch box (they travel brilliantly)
- Chop and add to a warm potato salad
