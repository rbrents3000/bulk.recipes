# Copycat Costco Tempura Shrimp Tacos

> Costco's newest deli hit, reverse-engineered for your kitchen. The cilantro lime crema is the real MVP.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 6 (12 tacos) | **Cost:** ~$3.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Frozen Raw Shrimp 21-25 ct — about 1 lb (~$18.99, 2 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Flour Tortillas — 12 small, or cut burrito-size in half (~$4.49, 24 count)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup cornstarch
- 1 cup ice-cold sparkling water
- Vegetable oil for frying (about 2 inches in a heavy pot)
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
- 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, finely chopped
- 2 cups green cabbage, shredded
- Pico de gallo for topping
- Salt to taste

## Instructions
1. Peel, devein, and butterfly the shrimp. Pat very dry and season lightly with salt.
2. Make the tempura batter: whisk flour and cornstarch together, then stir in ice-cold sparkling water. Keep it lumpy — overmixing is how you get dense, bready shrimp instead of crispy ones.
3. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pot to 375°F. Use a thermometer — guessing leads to greasy regret.
4. Dip each shrimp in batter, letting the excess drip off. Fry in batches of 4-5 for 2-3 minutes until golden and crispy. Drain on a wire rack, not paper towels.
5. Make the crema: stir together sour cream, lime juice, chopped cilantro, and a pinch of salt.
6. Warm tortillas in a dry skillet until pliable, about 30 seconds per side.
7. Assemble: tortilla, shredded cabbage, 2-3 tempura shrimp, a drizzle of crema, and a spoonful of pico de gallo.

> **Tip:** The sparkling water is what makes the batter impossibly light and crispy. Do NOT substitute flat water. This is the secret, and it's not negotiable.

## Storage
Eat immediately — tempura waits for nobody. The crema keeps up to 5 days in the fridge.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pile leftover shrimp on a hoagie roll with lettuce and crema for a shrimp po'boy
- Use extra crema as a dip for raw veggies or tortilla chips
- Shredded cabbage becomes slaw for any taco you make the rest of the week
