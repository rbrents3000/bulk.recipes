# Costco Food Court Combo Calzone

> Because the food court line is long, but your oven is right there.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Pepperoni — 4 oz (~$1.50, from 2-pack of 16 oz)
- Italian Sausage — 1/2 lb, casings removed (~$2.50, from 4-pack)
- Kirkland Shredded Mozzarella — 2 cups (~$2.50, from 2.5 lb bag)
- Rao's Marinara Sauce — 1 cup for filling + extra for dipping (~$5.00, from 2-pack)
- Organic Bell Peppers — 2 (any color) (~$2.00, from 6-pack)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 lbs pizza dough (store-bought, or 2 standard dough balls)
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
- Pinch of flaky salt

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook the sausage, breaking it into crumbles, until browned (about 5 minutes). Add diced peppers and onion, cooking 3-4 minutes more until softened. Drain any excess grease.
3. Divide the dough into 6 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a roughly 7-inch oval on a floured surface.
4. On one half of each oval, layer pepperoni, the sausage-pepper-onion mixture, mozzarella, and a spoonful of marinara. Sprinkle with Italian seasoning and garlic powder.
5. Fold the other half of the dough over the filling. Crimp the edges with a fork to seal. Cut 2-3 small slits on top for steam.
6. Place calzones on the baking sheets. Brush tops with beaten egg and sprinkle with flaky salt.
7. Bake for 22-25 minutes until the crust is golden brown and puffed.
8. Let cool for 5 minutes (the filling is lava). Serve with warm marinara for dipping.

> **Tip:** Don't overstuff the calzones — it's tempting, but they'll burst open in the oven and you'll have a pizza-flavored crime scene on your baking sheet.

## Storage
Store baked calzones wrapped individually in foil in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat in a 375F oven for 10-12 minutes. They also freeze beautifully — wrap in foil then a freezer bag for up to 3 months. Reheat from frozen at 375F for 20-25 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice in half and dip into soup for a hearty lunch
- Chop up and toss onto a salad for the world's most indulgent crouton substitute
- Wrap in foil and pack for road trip snacks — they travel like champs
