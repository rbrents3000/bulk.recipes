# Copycat Costco Double Chocolate Chunk Cookie

> The food court cookie that quietly became everyone's favorite impulse buy. $2.49 there. About fifty cents here. Do the math.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 12 min | **Servings:** 12 cookies | **Cost:** ~$0.50/cookie

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips — 2 cups (~$9.99, 4.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 1 cup / 2 sticks (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Cage-Free Eggs — 2 large (~$7.99, 5 dozen)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1/4 cup hot fudge sauce for drizzling *(optional)*
- Flaky sea salt for topping *(optional but highly recommended)*

## Instructions
1. Brown the butter: melt both sticks in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, swirling occasionally. It will foam, then sputter, then the milk solids will turn golden with nutty brown flecks settling on the bottom. That's when you pull it — pour immediately into a large mixing bowl.
2. Let the browned butter cool for about 5 minutes. You want it warm, not hot, or it'll melt your sugars into a greasy mess.
3. Add brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla to the browned butter. Whisk vigorously for about 2 minutes until smooth, slightly thickened, and a bit glossy.
4. Add eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. The mixture should look thick and ribbon-like.
5. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
6. Fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture with a spatula — stir just until the last streak of flour disappears. No more. Overmixing makes tough cookies.
7. Fold in the chocolate chips. The dough will be thick, dark, and incredibly rich-looking.
8. Scoop into large balls, about 3 ounces each (a heaping 1/4 cup). These are meant to be bakery-sized — don't be shy.
9. Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet with 3 inches between them. Sprinkle tops with flaky sea salt if using.
10. Bake at 350°F for 10-12 minutes. Pull them when the edges are set but the centers still look slightly underdone and puffy. They'll flatten and firm up as they cool.
11. Let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Or eat one warm right off the sheet pan — nobody's judging.
12. If you want to be extra, drizzle warm cookies with hot fudge and an additional pinch of sea salt.

> **Tip:** Browning the butter is what makes these taste like the food court version. It adds nutty, toffee-like depth that regular melted butter simply cannot match. Don't skip this step — it's the whole reason this recipe works.

> **Tip:** For even better cookies, scoop the dough balls and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 3 days) before baking. Cold dough spreads slower and develops chewier edges.

## Storage
Keep in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days — if they last that long. For future cookie emergencies, freeze unbaked dough balls on a sheet pan until solid, then transfer to a freezer bag. They keep for up to 3 months. Bake from frozen at 350°F for 13-15 minutes — no need to thaw.

## Leftover Ideas
- Sandwich a scoop of vanilla ice cream between two cookies for the ultimate ice cream sandwich
- Crumble over a bowl of ice cream for the world's best sundae topping
- Warm a cookie in the microwave for 10 seconds and pretend it just came out of the oven
- Crumble into the bottom of a glass, layer with whipped cream and chocolate pudding for a trifle
- Package a few in a bag with a ribbon — homemade cookies are the best hostess gift, and these look impressive
- Crumble over a milkshake for the most over-the-top dessert drink you've ever had
