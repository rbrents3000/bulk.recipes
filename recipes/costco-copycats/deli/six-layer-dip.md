# Copycat Costco Six-Layer Dip

> The Costco deli's six-layer dip is a party staple — beans, guacamole, sour cream, salsa, cheese, and olives stacked in a tray. Yours will be fresher, bigger, and you won't have to fight over the last scoop.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 12 | **Cost:** ~$1.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Organic Salsa — 1 cup (~$7.99, 2-pack/38 oz)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Mexican Blend Cheese — 1.5 cups (~$12.99, 2.5 lb bag)
- Hass Avocados — 3 (~$6.99, 5-pack)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Tortilla Chips — for serving (~$4.99, 40 oz bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 can (16 oz) refried beans
- 1 cup sour cream
- 1 packet taco seasoning (or 2 tablespoons homemade)
- 1 can (2.25 oz) sliced black olives, drained
- 3 green onions, thinly sliced
- Juice of 1 lime
- Salt to taste

## Instructions
1. **Layer 1 — Beans:** Mix refried beans with half the taco seasoning. Spread evenly across the bottom of a 9x13 dish or large platter.
2. **Layer 2 — Sour cream:** Mix sour cream with remaining taco seasoning. Spread over the beans.
3. **Layer 3 — Guacamole:** Mash avocados with lime juice and salt. Spread over the sour cream.
4. **Layer 4 — Salsa:** Spoon salsa evenly over the guacamole.
5. **Layer 5 — Cheese:** Sprinkle shredded Mexican blend across the top.
6. **Layer 6 — Olives:** Scatter olives and green onions over everything.
7. Serve with tortilla chips.

> **Tip:** Press plastic wrap directly onto the guacamole layer if you're making this ahead — it stops it from browning.

## Storage
Best eaten within 24 hours. Refrigerate covered with plastic wrap pressed against the surface. The guac layer will brown by day 2 no matter what.

## Leftover Ideas
- Scoop into a burrito with rice and chicken
- Spread on a tortilla and roll up for a quick wrap
