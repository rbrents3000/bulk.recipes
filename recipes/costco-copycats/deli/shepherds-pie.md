# Copycat Costco Shepherd's Pie

> Savory ground beef and vegetables under a thick blanket of creamy mashed potatoes — the Costco deli version in a microwave tray doesn't hold a candle to this one straight from your oven.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 35 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef 88/12 — 2 lbs (~$6.99/lb, 4-pack patties or bulk tube)
- Russet Potatoes — 3 lbs (about 6 large) (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 6 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Frozen Mixed Vegetables — 2 cups (~$7.49, 5.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Sharp Cheddar — 1 cup (~$14.99, 2 lb block)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/2 cup milk or heavy cream
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 cup beef broth
- 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Peel and cube potatoes. Boil in salted water until fork-tender, about 15 minutes. Drain, then mash with butter, milk, and half the cheddar. Season with salt and pepper.
3. While potatoes cook, brown ground beef in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Drain excess fat.
4. Add onion and cook 3-4 minutes. Add garlic, tomato paste, thyme, and flour. Stir for 1 minute.
5. Pour in beef broth and Worcestershire sauce. Simmer 5 minutes until thickened. Stir in frozen vegetables and cook 2 minutes more. Season with salt and pepper.
6. Spread mashed potatoes evenly over the meat filling. Use a fork to create ridges on top (they get crispy). Sprinkle with remaining cheddar.
7. Bake 20-25 minutes until the top is golden and the edges are bubbling.

> **Tip:** Broil for the last 2 minutes if you want those potato peaks extra golden. Watch it closely.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat individual portions in the microwave or reheat the whole dish at 350°F for 20 minutes. Freezes well for up to 3 months.

## Leftover Ideas
- Scoop into a bowl and top with a fried egg for breakfast
- Roll spoonfuls into balls, bread them, and deep-fry for shepherd's pie croquettes
