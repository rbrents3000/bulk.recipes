# Copycat Costco Loaded Baked Potatoes

> Big, fluffy baked potatoes stuffed with cheese, bacon, and sour cream — the Costco deli sells 6 for $12 but you can make them better and cheaper with a bag of russets and some patience.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 60 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$1.75/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Russet Potatoes — 6 large (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Thick-Sliced Bacon — 8 slices (~$18.99, 4 x 1 lb packs)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Sharp Cheddar — 1.5 cups (~$14.99, 2 lb block)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 4 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 4 green onions, thinly sliced
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Scrub potatoes, prick all over with a fork, and rub with a little olive oil and salt.
2. Place directly on the oven rack and bake 50-60 minutes until a knife slides in easily.
3. While potatoes bake, cook bacon until crispy. Crumble or chop into bits.
4. When potatoes are done, slice the top open lengthwise. Squeeze the sides to fluff up the inside.
5. Add a pat of butter to each potato, then load with cheddar, bacon bits, a dollop of sour cream, and green onions.
6. Return to oven for 5 minutes if you want the cheese fully melted.

> **Tip:** For extra-crispy skin, rub with oil and salt before baking. For extra-fluffy insides, bake directly on the rack — no foil.

## Storage
Bake all potatoes and refrigerate plain (without toppings) up to 5 days. Reheat at 350°F for 15 minutes, then load fresh toppings.

## Leftover Ideas
- Scoop out the insides and make loaded mashed potatoes
- Dice leftover baked potato and fry in a skillet for home fries
- Chop and add to a breakfast burrito
