# Copycat Costco Clam Chowder

> Thick, creamy New England clam chowder loaded with potatoes and tender clams — tastes like the Costco deli version but you made a whole pot for the same price as their one container.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 30 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Thick-Sliced Bacon — 6 slices (~$18.99, 4 x 1 lb packs)
- Russet Potatoes — 1.5 lbs (about 3 medium), peeled and cubed (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 3 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Heavy Cream — 1 cup (~$5.99, 2-quart carton)

## Other Ingredients
- 3 cans (6.5 oz each) chopped clams, drained (reserve the juice)
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2 stalks celery, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2 cups clam juice (from cans + bottled if needed)
- 1 cup whole milk
- 2 bay leaves
- 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh chives or parsley for garnish *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Cook bacon in a large Dutch oven over medium heat until crispy. Remove and crumble. Leave 2 tablespoons of fat in the pot.
2. Add butter, onion, and celery. Cook 5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
3. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir for 2 minutes to cook out the raw taste.
4. Slowly pour in clam juice while stirring to prevent lumps. Add potatoes, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a simmer and cook 15 minutes until potatoes are tender.
5. Stir in heavy cream, milk, and clams. Heat through for 5 minutes — don't let it boil or the clams get rubbery.
6. Remove bay leaves. Season with salt and pepper. Serve topped with crumbled bacon and chives.

> **Tip:** Let it sit for 10 minutes before serving — chowder thickens as it rests and the flavors come together.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 3 days. Reheat gently over low heat, stirring often. Don't freeze — the cream and potatoes don't hold up.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour into bread bowls for a restaurant-style presentation
- Thin with a little broth and toss with pasta for clam chowder pasta
