# Copycat Costco Crab Cakes

> Golden, crispy crab cakes packed with real crab — not breadcrumb filler. The deli ones are $15 for 8 and they're cold. Yours will be hot out of the skillet and twice as good.

**Prep:** 15 min + 30 min chill | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 8 cakes | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Canned Crab Meat — 1 lb lump crab (~$14.99, 2-pack/6 oz cans) or fresh lump crab from seafood counter
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 1 egg (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Kirkland Signature Mayonnaise — 3 tablespoons (~$8.99, 64 oz jar)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 2 tablespoons (~$13.99, 2 L)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
- 2 green onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
- Pinch of cayenne pepper
- Salt to taste
- 2 tablespoons butter (for pan-frying)
- Lemon wedges and tartar sauce for serving

## Instructions
1. Pick through the crab meat and remove any shell fragments. Gently squeeze out excess moisture with paper towels — don't crush it.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together egg, mayonnaise, Dijon, Worcestershire, Old Bay, lemon juice, cayenne, and a pinch of salt.
3. Fold in crab meat, green onions, and panko breadcrumbs. Mix gently — you want chunks of crab, not a paste.
4. Form into 8 patties about 3 inches wide and 3/4 inch thick. Place on a parchment-lined plate and refrigerate 30 minutes (this keeps them from falling apart).
5. Heat olive oil and butter in a large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Cook crab cakes 3-4 minutes per side until deep golden brown.
6. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and tartar sauce.

> **Tip:** Resist the urge to press down on them while they cook. Let the crust form, then flip once.

## Storage
Refrigerate cooked crab cakes up to 2 days. Reheat in a 375°F oven for 8-10 minutes. You can also freeze the uncooked patties — place on a sheet pan to freeze solid, then transfer to a zip-lock bag for up to 2 months.

## Leftover Ideas
- Crumble over a salad with lemon vinaigrette
- Make a crab cake sandwich on a brioche bun with slaw and remoulade
- Break into pieces and fold into scrambled eggs
