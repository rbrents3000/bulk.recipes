# Copycat Costco Seasoned Chicken Wings

> The deli's seasoned wings come in three flavors. Here's all three — buffalo, teriyaki, and garlic Parmesan — so you can make a platter that beats the cold deli tray every time.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$3.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Chicken Wings — 3 lbs (~$3.49/lb, 10 lb bag frozen)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 4 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Parmigiano Reggiano — 1/2 cup (~$14.99, 1 lb tub)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 2 tablespoons (~$13.99, 2 L)

## Other Ingredients
**For all wings:**
- 1 tablespoon baking powder (not baking soda)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon pepper

**Buffalo sauce:**
- 1/2 cup Frank's RedHot
- 2 tablespoons melted butter

**Teriyaki glaze:**
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch + 1 tablespoon water

**Garlic Parmesan:**
- 3 tablespoons melted butter
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan
- 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with foil and set a wire rack on top.
2. Pat wings completely dry with paper towels. Toss with baking powder, salt, and pepper.
3. Arrange wings in a single layer on the rack. Bake 45 minutes, flipping halfway, until skin is deeply golden and crispy.
4. While wings bake, make your sauce(s):
   - **Buffalo:** Melt butter, whisk in hot sauce. Done.
   - **Teriyaki:** Combine soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and garlic in a small saucepan. Simmer 3 minutes. Mix cornstarch slurry, add to pan, stir until thickened.
   - **Garlic Parm:** Melt butter in a skillet, cook garlic 1 minute, remove from heat. Toss with Parmesan and parsley after coating wings.
5. Toss hot wings in your sauce of choice. Serve immediately.

> **Tip:** Baking powder on the skin is the secret to crispy oven wings — it raises the pH and helps the skin blister and crunch without frying.

## Storage
Refrigerate sauced wings up to 3 days. Reheat in a 400°F oven for 10 minutes to re-crisp. Don't microwave — soggy wings are a crime.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pull the meat and toss into a salad
- Chop buffalo wings and stuff into a grilled cheese
- Shred teriyaki wings over rice for a quick bowl
