# Copycat Costco Mashed Potatoes

> Rich, creamy mashed potatoes that taste just like the Costco deli tub — except you made them fresh, they're still steaming, and you didn't have to fight for parking. The deli sells ~3 lbs for $8.99, but a bag of russets and some Kirkland butter gets you there for a fraction of the price.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$1.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Russet or Gold Potatoes — 4 lbs (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 6 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Heavy Cream — 3/4 cup (~$11.49, half gallon)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper or black pepper
- 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)

## Instructions
1. Peel potatoes and cut into roughly 1-inch chunks — keep them uniform so they cook evenly.
2. Place potatoes in a large pot and cover with cold, salted water by about an inch.
3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a steady simmer. Cook 15-20 minutes until a fork slides through with zero resistance.
4. While potatoes simmer, warm the heavy cream and butter together in a small saucepan over low heat until the butter melts. If using garlic, saut&eacute; it in the butter for 1 minute before adding the cream.
5. Drain potatoes thoroughly and return them to the hot pot. Let them sit for 1-2 minutes to steam off excess moisture — this is the secret to non-gluey mash.
6. Mash with a potato masher or ricer. Pour in the warm cream-butter mixture and stir gently until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper.

> **Tip:** Use a potato ricer instead of a masher for the silkiest, lump-free texture — it's what gives that deli-smooth finish. Never use a food processor or blender unless you want potato glue.

> **Tip:** Warming the cream and butter before adding them keeps the potatoes fluffy. Cold dairy tightens up the starch and makes everything gummy.

## Storage
Transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate up to 5 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of cream or milk, stirring often. Microwave works too — cover and heat in 1-minute intervals, stirring between.

## Leftover Ideas
- Form into patties, coat in panko, and pan-fry for crispy potato cakes
- Spread as the topping for a shepherd's pie or cottage pie
- Stir in cheddar and chives, roll into balls, and bake for loaded potato bites
