# Copycat Costco Meatloaf

> A big, juicy meatloaf with a sticky-sweet glaze — just like the one in the Costco deli case but yours won't have that sad plastic wrap tan line. Feeds 8 and costs a fraction of the $18 deli price.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 60 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef 88/12 — 2.5 lbs (~$6.99/lb, 4-pack patties or bulk tube)
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 2 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Kirkland Signature Ketchup — 1/2 cup + 1/4 cup for glaze (~$4.99, 2-pack/40 oz)

## Other Ingredients
- 3/4 cup plain breadcrumbs
- 1/2 cup whole milk
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon pepper
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar (for glaze)
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard (for glaze)

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with foil.
2. Soak breadcrumbs in milk for 5 minutes.
3. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, soaked breadcrumbs, eggs, onion, garlic, 1/2 cup ketchup, Worcestershire, thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix with your hands until just combined — don't overwork it or the meatloaf gets dense.
4. Shape into a loaf about 10 inches long on the prepared baking sheet.
5. Mix the glaze: 1/4 cup ketchup, brown sugar, and Dijon mustard. Spread over the top of the meatloaf.
6. Bake 55-65 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 160°F. Let rest 10 minutes before slicing.

> **Tip:** Shape it on the sheet pan, not in a loaf pan — the exposed sides get a crust that's half the appeal.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freezes well for up to 3 months — wrap tightly in plastic and foil.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice and make meatloaf sandwiches on Kirkland brioche buns
- Crumble into pasta sauce for a quick bolognese
- Dice and fry in a skillet with eggs for a diner-style breakfast
