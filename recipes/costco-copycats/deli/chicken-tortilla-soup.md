# Copycat Costco Chicken Tortilla Soup

> Smoky, spicy Southwest-style chicken tortilla soup with a rich cumin-chili broth, shredded rotisserie chicken, and all the toppings — better than the Costco deli container and you'll get four times the soup for the same money.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 30 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Rotisserie Chicken — 1 whole chicken, shredded (~$4.99)
- Kirkland Signature Chicken Broth — 8 cups (~$4.99, 6-pack cartons)
- Kirkland Signature Canned Diced Tomatoes — 1 can (28 oz), undrained (~$6.49, 8-pack)
- Kirkland Signature Sweet Corn — 1 can (15 oz), drained (~$6.89, 12-pack)
- Avocados — 2, diced for topping (~$6.99, 5-count bag)
- Limes — 2, cut into wedges (~$4.99, 5 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 1 poblano pepper, seeded and diced
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Tortilla strips for topping
- Sour cream for topping *(optional)*
- Shredded cheese for topping *(optional)*
- Fresh cilantro for garnish *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or stockpot over medium-high heat. Add onion, poblano, and jalapeño. Cook 5 minutes until softened.
2. Add garlic, cumin, chili powder, and smoked paprika. Stir for 1 minute until fragrant — this blooms the spices and builds that smoky backbone.
3. Pour in chicken broth and diced tomatoes with their juices. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
4. Add shredded rotisserie chicken, corn, and black beans. Simmer 20 minutes to let everything meld together.
5. Season with salt and pepper. Squeeze in the juice of one lime and stir.
6. Ladle into bowls and pile on the toppings: tortilla strips, diced avocado, a squeeze of lime, sour cream, cheese, and cilantro.

> **Tip:** For extra depth, char the poblano and jalapeño under the broiler before dicing. And if you want it thicker, smash some of the black beans with a fork before adding them — instant body without any flour.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freezes great for up to 3 months — leave out the toppings and add them fresh when you reheat. The broth actually gets better after a day in the fridge.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour over rice for a quick burrito bowl situation
- Use as the filling for enchiladas — just reduce it down a bit so it's thicker
- Stir in cooked pasta and extra cheese for a Tex-Mex chicken noodle soup
