# Copycat Costco Beef Chili

> The grab-and-go chili from the Costco deli is hearty, beefy, and just spicy enough. This makes a full pot for the price of two of their containers — and your kitchen will smell incredible.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Servings:** 10 | **Cost:** ~$2.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef 88/12 — 2.5 lbs (~$6.99/lb, 4-pack or bulk)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Diced Tomatoes — 2 cans, 14.5 oz each (~$1.50 each, 8-pack)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Kidney Beans — 2 cans, 15 oz each (~$1.00 each, from variety pack)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Sharp Cheddar — 1 cup for topping (~$14.99, 2 lb block)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large onion, diced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
- 2 cups beef broth
- 3 tablespoons chili powder
- 1 tablespoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Sour cream and green onions for topping *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Brown ground beef in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, breaking it into small pieces. Drain excess fat, leaving about 1 tablespoon in the pot.
2. Add onion and bell pepper. Cook 5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
3. Stir in chili powder, cumin, paprika, and cayenne. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Add tomato paste and stir for 2 minutes. Pour in diced tomatoes, kidney beans (drained and rinsed), beef broth, and Worcestershire sauce.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cook uncovered 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened.
6. Season with salt and pepper. Serve topped with shredded cheddar, sour cream, and green onions.

> **Tip:** Chili is always better the next day. Make it ahead and let the flavors meld overnight in the fridge.

*For a quicker weeknight version, see the [Dump-and-Go Chili](../../weeknight-dinners/dump-and-go-chili.md) — 30 minutes, no browning step.*

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 5 days. Freezes perfectly for up to 4 months. One of the best batch-cooking recipes there is.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour over hot dogs for chili dogs
- Spoon over baked potatoes with cheese and sour cream
- Use as filling for chili cheese nachos
