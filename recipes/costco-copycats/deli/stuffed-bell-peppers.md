# Copycat Costco Stuffed Bell Peppers

> Six beefy, cheesy stuffed peppers just like the ones in the Costco deli case — except yours come out of the oven hot and bubbling instead of lukewarm under plastic wrap.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.75/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef 88/12 — 1.5 lbs (~$6.99/lb, 4-pack patties or bulk tube)
- Bell Peppers — 6 large (~$6.99, 6-pack)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Mexican Blend Cheese — 1.5 cups (~$12.99, 2.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Diced Tomatoes — 1 can, 14.5 oz (~$1.50, 8-pack)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 cup cooked white rice
- 1/2 medium onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1/2 cup tomato sauce

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove seeds. Stand them upright in a baking dish.
2. Brown ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it into small pieces. Drain excess fat.
3. Add onion and cook 3-4 minutes until softened. Add garlic, Italian seasoning, and paprika. Cook 1 minute.
4. Stir in diced tomatoes, cooked rice, and half the cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Stuff each pepper generously with the beef mixture, mounding it on top. Pour tomato sauce into the bottom of the baking dish and add 1/4 cup water.
6. Cover tightly with foil and bake 30 minutes. Remove foil, top each pepper with remaining cheese, and bake uncovered 10-15 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly.

> **Tip:** Par-cook the peppers in boiling water for 3 minutes if you like them softer. Skip this if you prefer some bite.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freeze unbaked (stuffed but not cooked) for up to 3 months — bake from frozen, adding 15-20 minutes to the cook time.

## Leftover Ideas
- Chop up a leftover pepper and scramble it into eggs
- Scoop out the filling and use it as taco meat
- Slice in half and serve open-faced on toast
