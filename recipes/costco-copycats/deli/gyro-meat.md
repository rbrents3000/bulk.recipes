# Copycat Costco Gyro Meat

> The deli gyro kit is convenient but the meat is the star — here's how to make it from scratch with ground lamb and beef so you never need the kit again. Crispy edges, warm spices, the whole deal.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Ground Lamb — 1 lb (~$7.99/lb, from meat counter)
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef 88/12 — 1 lb (~$6.99/lb, 4-pack or bulk)
- Kirkland Signature Feta Cheese — 1/2 cup crumbled (~$7.99, 24 oz container)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Cucumber — 1 large (~$1.00, 3-pack)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 small onion, roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon coriander
- 1/2 teaspoon rosemary
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1 teaspoon salt
- Pita bread for serving
- Tzatziki sauce (store-bought or homemade)
- 2 tomatoes, diced
- Red onion, thinly sliced

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C).
2. Pulse onion and garlic in a food processor until very finely minced — almost a paste.
3. Add ground lamb, ground beef, oregano, cumin, coriander, rosemary, salt, and pepper to the food processor. Process for about 1 minute until the mixture is very smooth and sticky — this is what gives gyro meat its dense texture.
4. Pack the mixture tightly into a loaf pan, pressing out all air pockets. Cover tightly with foil.
5. Place the loaf pan inside a larger baking dish. Fill the outer dish with boiling water halfway up the sides of the loaf pan (water bath).
6. Bake 45 minutes until internal temperature reaches 165°F.
7. Remove from oven, drain any fat, and let rest 15 minutes. Refrigerate at least 1 hour (overnight is better) — the loaf firms up for slicing.
8. Slice the loaf as thin as possible. Sear slices in a hot skillet with a little oil until crispy on the edges.

> **Tip:** The food processor step is non-negotiable. You want a paste, not a meatloaf. That's what gives real gyro meat its tight, sliceable texture.

## Storage
Refrigerate the unsliced loaf up to 5 days. Slice and sear as needed. Freezes great for up to 3 months — slice first, then freeze flat.

## Leftover Ideas
- Chop and toss into a rice bowl with tzatziki, cucumber, and feta
- Dice and fold into an omelet with feta and tomatoes
- Stuff into a pita with hummus for a quick lunch
