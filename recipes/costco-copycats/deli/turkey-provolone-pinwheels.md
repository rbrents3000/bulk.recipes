# Copycat Costco Turkey Provolone Pinwheels

> The deli sells 20 pinwheels for $10 and they vanish at every party. These are dead simple — roll, slice, done. Make them the night before and you're a hero.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 20 pinwheels | **Cost:** ~$0.50/pinwheel

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Oven Roasted Turkey Breast — 1 lb, thinly sliced (~$11.99, 2 lb pack)
- Provolone Cheese — 8 slices (~$8.99, 2 lb pack)
- Kirkland Signature Flour Tortillas — 4 large (~$4.49, 24 count)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Spring Mix — 2 cups (~$4.99, 1 lb clamshell)

## Other Ingredients
- 4 tablespoons cream cheese, softened
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Mix cream cheese, Dijon mustard, and garlic powder until smooth.
2. Spread a thin layer of the cream cheese mixture across each tortilla, edge to edge.
3. Layer turkey slices, provolone, and spring mix evenly across each tortilla, leaving a 1-inch border at the top edge.
4. Roll each tortilla tightly from the bottom, keeping it as snug as possible. The cream cheese acts as glue.
5. Wrap each roll tightly in plastic wrap. Refrigerate at least 1 hour (overnight is best — they firm up and slice cleaner).
6. Unwrap and trim the uneven ends. Slice each roll into 5 pinwheels.

> **Tip:** Wet the top border of the tortilla with a finger dipped in water before finishing the roll — it seals better.

## Storage
Refrigerate wrapped rolls up to 3 days. Slice just before serving for the cleanest presentation. Don't freeze — the lettuce turns to mush.

## Leftover Ideas
- Serve unsliced as wraps for a quick lunch
- Skip the lettuce and pack in a lunchbox — holds up better
