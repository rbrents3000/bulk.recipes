# Chicken & Waffles

> The deli kit that broke the internet, now in your kitchen with better syrup.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$3.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs — 1.5 lbs / 6 thighs (~$2.99/lb)
- Kirkland Signature Cage-Free Eggs — 2 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Kirkland Signature Honey — 2 tbsp (~$12.99, 5 lb jug)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Oil for frying (vegetable or peanut)
- 4 frozen waffles *(or make from scratch if you're feeling ambitious)*
- Maple syrup
- Hot sauce (Frank's RedHot)

## Instructions
1. Set up your dredging station: mix flour, paprika, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, and pepper in one bowl. Beat eggs in another.
2. Dip each chicken thigh in egg, then seasoned flour, then back in egg, then flour again. This is the double-dredge — don't rush it.
3. Heat oil to 350°F in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven. You want at least 2 inches of oil.
4. Fry chicken 6-8 minutes per side until deep golden brown and 165°F internal. Work in batches — don't crowd the pot.
5. Rest chicken on a wire rack (not paper towels — you want air circulating underneath to keep the bottom crispy).
6. While chicken rests, toast your waffles until golden and crisp.
7. Mix 2 tablespoons honey with a few shakes of hot sauce for the hot honey drizzle.
8. Stack chicken on waffles. Drizzle with maple syrup and hot honey. Try not to make any embarrassing noises.

> **Tip:** The double-dredge is what gives the coating that thick, craggy texture. Don't skip the second round — it's the difference between fast food and food court legend.

## Storage
Fried chicken keeps 3 days in the fridge. Reheat in a 375°F oven for 10 minutes to re-crisp. Do not microwave unless you enjoy soggy breading.

## Leftover Ideas
- Chicken sandwich on a buttermilk biscuit with pickles
- Slice into strips for a fried chicken salad
- Waffle breakfast sandwich with a fried egg and melted cheese
