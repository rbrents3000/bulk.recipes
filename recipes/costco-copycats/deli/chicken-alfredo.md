# Costco Deli Chicken Alfredo

> The deli tray you've been buying for $14.99, except now you can make it for half that and triple the garlic.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Boneless Skinless Chicken Breasts — 1.5 lbs (~$3.49/lb, from 6.5 lb pack)
- Kirkland Signature Parmigiano Reggiano — 1 cup grated (~$16.99, wedge)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 3 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Heavy Whipping Cream — 2 cups (~$11.99, half gallon)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 lb penne pasta
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg *(optional, but highly recommended)*
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish
- Red pepper flakes *(optional, for a little kick)*

## Instructions
1. Season chicken breasts generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
2. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat with the olive oil. Sear chicken 5-6 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Internal temp should hit 165°F.
3. Remove chicken from the skillet and let it rest for 5 minutes, then slice into thin strips. Resist the urge to cut it immediately — resting keeps the juices in the chicken, not on your cutting board.
4. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a rolling boil. Cook penne according to package directions until al dente — a minute less than the box says is usually perfect.
5. Before draining, reserve 1/2 cup pasta water. This starchy liquid is your secret weapon for sauce consistency.
6. While the pasta cooks, start the sauce. In the same skillet over medium heat, melt butter. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant — watch it like a hawk, burnt garlic is bitter garlic.
7. Pour in heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Let it reduce for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it starts to coat the back of a spoon.
8. Remove from heat and stir in the grated Parmigiano Reggiano a handful at a time until the sauce is silky smooth. Add a pinch of nutmeg if using.
9. If the sauce feels too thick, add a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen it up. A little goes a long way.
10. Toss the drained pasta and sliced chicken into the sauce. Stir until everything is evenly coated. The pasta will continue absorbing sauce as it sits, so err on the saucier side.
11. Plate immediately, top with fresh parsley, a crack of black pepper, and red pepper flakes if you like a little heat.

> **Tip:** Adding the parm off the heat prevents it from clumping. If your sauce gets grainy, a splash of hot pasta water and vigorous stirring will usually save it.

> **Tip:** Real parm (not the green can) is what makes this taste like the deli version. Grate it yourself — pre-shredded has anti-caking agents that prevent smooth melting. A Microplane gives you the fluffiest, meltiest shreds.

## Storage
Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of cream or milk — microwaving alfredo tends to break the sauce into a greasy mess. Stir constantly while reheating and it'll come back together beautifully.

Not recommended for freezing — cream-based pasta sauces don't thaw gracefully. If you want to meal prep, freeze the cooked chicken separately and make the sauce fresh.

## Leftover Ideas
- Stuff it into a split garlic bread loaf and broil until bubbly for an alfredo boat
- Toss with steamed broccoli florets, pour into a baking dish, top with extra parm, and bake at 375°F for a quick alfredo bake
- Roll into flour tortillas with extra parm and spinach for creamy alfredo wraps
- Spread in a baking dish, crack eggs on top, and bake for an alfredo shakshuka brunch
- Stir into risotto for a ridiculously rich chicken alfredo risotto
- Chop the chicken, mix with the pasta, and stuff into bell peppers for alfredo stuffed peppers
