# Copycat Costco Chicken Noodle Soup

> The classic Costco deli chicken noodle soup — rich golden broth, shredded rotisserie chicken, tender egg noodles, and big chunks of carrot. You'll get a full pot for what they charge for one sad little container.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$1.75/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Rotisserie Chicken — 1 whole, shredded (~$4.99 each)
- Kirkland Signature Chicken Broth — 8 cups (~$11.49, 6-pack of 32 oz cartons)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Carrots — 4 large, peeled and sliced (~$4.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Celery — 4 stalks, sliced (~$5.99, 2-pack hearts)

## Other Ingredients
- 12 oz wide egg noodles
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Cook 5 minutes until the onion is translucent and the veggies start to soften.
2. Add garlic, thyme, and rosemary. Stir for about 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in all 8 cups of chicken broth. Drop in the bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer for 10 minutes until the carrots are just tender.
4. Add the egg noodles and cook 7–8 minutes until al dente. The noodles will soak up broth, so don't skip any liquid.
5. Stir in the shredded rotisserie chicken and heat through for 2–3 minutes.
6. Remove bay leaves. Season generously with salt and pepper. Ladle into bowls and hit it with fresh parsley if you're feeling fancy.

> **Tip:** Shred the rotisserie chicken while it's still warm — it pulls apart way easier. Dark meat adds more flavor, so don't leave those thighs behind.

> **Tip:** If you want that deep golden color like the Costco version, add a pinch of turmeric with the spices. It won't change the flavor but it'll look the part.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. The noodles will soak up broth overnight — add a splash of extra broth when reheating. Freezes well for up to 3 months if you leave the noodles out (cook them fresh when you reheat).

## Leftover Ideas
- Ladle over biscuits for a chicken noodle pot pie vibe
- Toss the solids with butter and Parmesan for a quick chicken noodle skillet
- Serve in a bread bowl — Costco bakery sourdough rounds work perfectly
