# Copycat Costco Broccoli Cheddar Soup

> Thick, velvety broccoli cheddar soup that tastes like the Costco deli container — except you get a full pot for the price of two of theirs.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Broccoli Florets — 4 cups (~$5.99, 4 lb bag frozen)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Sharp Cheddar — 2 cups (~$14.99, 2 lb block)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 4 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Heavy Cream — 1 cup (~$5.99, 2-quart carton)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
- 3 cups chicken broth
- 1 cup whole milk
- 1/2 teaspoon dry mustard powder
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and cook 4-5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute.
2. Sprinkle flour over the onions and stir constantly for 2 minutes to make a roux.
3. Slowly whisk in chicken broth and milk, stirring to prevent lumps. Bring to a simmer.
4. Add broccoli florets, mustard powder, and nutmeg. Simmer 12-15 minutes until broccoli is very tender.
5. Use an immersion blender to blend about half the soup (leave it chunky). Or transfer half to a blender, blend smooth, and return to the pot.
6. Stir in heavy cream and cheddar cheese. Stir until cheese is fully melted and smooth. Season with salt and pepper.

> **Tip:** The sharp cheddar is doing all the heavy lifting here — don't substitute mild. And grate it yourself from a block for the smoothest melt. Pre-shredded has anti-caking starch that can make the soup grainy.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat gently over low heat, stirring often. Add a splash of broth if it gets too thick.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour over baked potatoes for a loaded broccoli cheddar potato
- Use as a sauce for mac and cheese
- Serve in bread bowls
