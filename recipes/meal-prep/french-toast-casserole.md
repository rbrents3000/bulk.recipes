# French Toast Casserole

> Tear up Costco croissants, drown them in custard overnight, and wake up to the most indulgent breakfast that basically made itself.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Butter Croissants — 8 croissants (~$5.99, 12 count)
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 8 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Kirkland Signature Heavy Cream — 1 cup (~$9.99, half gallon)
- Kirkland Signature Maple Syrup — for serving (~$14.99, 33.8 oz bottle)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 2 tablespoons for greasing (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 cup Whole milk
- 1/3 cup Brown sugar
- 2 teaspoons Vanilla extract
- 1.5 teaspoons Cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon Nutmeg
- pinch Salt
- Powdered sugar for dusting *(optional)*
- Fresh berries for topping *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish generously.
2. Tear croissants into rough 1.5-inch chunks. Scatter them evenly in the baking dish — it's fine to pile them up a bit.
3. Whisk together eggs, heavy cream, milk, brown sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt until smooth.
4. Pour the custard mixture evenly over the croissant pieces. Press the chunks down gently with a spatula so they soak up the liquid.
5. Cover tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight (or at least 4 hours).
6. In the morning: preheat oven to 350°F. Remove the casserole from the fridge and let it sit at room temperature while the oven heats.
7. Bake uncovered for 40–45 minutes until the top is golden brown, puffed up, and the center is set (not jiggly).
8. Let cool 5 minutes. Dust with powdered sugar if you're feeling fancy. Serve with a generous pour of Kirkland maple syrup.

> **Tip:** Day-old croissants actually work better here — they soak up the custard without turning to mush. So if your croissants are fresh, tear them up and let them sit out for a few hours first.

## Storage
Cover and refrigerate leftovers up to 3 days. Reheat individual portions in the microwave for 60–90 seconds or in a 325°F oven for 10 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Cut into squares and pan-fry in butter for crispy edges
- Serve a cold square topped with Greek yogurt and berries
- Cube and use as a topping for ice cream (trust the process)
