# Overnight Oats

> Five jars of creamy, no-cook overnight oats loaded with Greek yogurt and mixed berries -- Sunday night prep, weekday breakfast on autopilot.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 5 | **Cost:** ~$1.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Rolled Oats — 2.5 cups (~$0.80, 10 lb bag at ~$8.99)
- Kirkland Signature Greek Yogurt — 1.25 cups (~$1.50, 3-pack 32 oz tubs)
- Kirkland Signature Raw Honey — 5 tbsp (~$0.60, 5 lb jug)
- Kirkland Signature Frozen Mixed Berries — 2.5 cups (~$2.50, 4 lb bag at ~$9.99)

## Other Ingredients
- 2.5 cups milk (dairy or plant-based)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt
- Optional toppings: chia seeds, sliced almonds, peanut butter, banana

## Instructions
1. Set out 5 mason jars or airtight containers (16 oz size works best).
2. Add 1/2 cup rolled oats to each jar.
3. Spoon 1/4 cup Greek yogurt into each jar.
4. Pour 1/2 cup milk into each jar.
5. Drizzle 1 tablespoon of honey into each jar. Add a small splash of vanilla and a tiny pinch of salt.
6. Stir each jar well until everything is combined. You want the oats fully submerged in the liquid.
7. Top each jar with 1/2 cup of frozen mixed berries. Don't stir them in -- they'll thaw overnight and create a nice fruity layer on top.
8. Seal the jars and refrigerate overnight, or for at least 4 hours.
9. In the morning, stir everything together and eat cold, or microwave for 1-2 minutes if you prefer warm oats.

> **Tip:** The oats thicken as they sit. If they're too thick by day 3 or 4, just stir in a splash of milk to loosen them up. You can also swap the berries for sliced bananas and a drizzle of peanut butter for a PB&B version.

## Storage
Keeps in the fridge for up to 5 days, so your whole work week is covered. Do not freeze -- the texture gets grainy after thawing.

## Leftover Ideas
- Blend a jar with a frozen banana and extra milk for a quick smoothie
- Warm up the oats and spread them in a baking dish with brown sugar on top for a lazy baked oatmeal
- Stir in a scoop of protein powder after thawing for a post-workout meal
