# Breakfast Burritos

> A freezer full of these is basically a cheat code for mornings. Grab, nuke, conquer the day.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 12 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Large Eggs — 12 eggs (~$7.99, 5 dozen)
- Kirkland Signature Italian Sausage Links — 1 lb, casings removed and crumbled (~$14.99, 6-pack)
- Bell Peppers — 2 peppers, diced (~$6.99, 6-pack)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Mexican Blend Cheese — 2 cups (~$12.99, 2.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Flour Tortillas — 12 tortillas (~$4.49, 2-pack of 10 count)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 tablespoon Olive oil or butter
- 1/2 teaspoon Salt
- 1/4 teaspoon Black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon Garlic powder
- to taste Hot sauce *(optional)*
- to taste Salsa for serving *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook crumbled sausage, breaking it into small pieces, until browned and cooked through — about 7 minutes. Transfer to a bowl.
2. In the same skillet (drain excess grease if needed), add a drizzle of oil. Sauté diced bell peppers for 3 minutes until slightly softened.
3. Whisk eggs with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Pour into the skillet with the peppers. Scramble over medium heat until just set — keep them slightly soft since they'll get reheated later.
4. Combine scrambled eggs, cooked sausage, and cheese in a large bowl. Toss gently.
5. Warm tortillas in the microwave for 15 seconds each (makes them pliable and less likely to tear).
6. Scoop about 1/2 cup of filling onto each tortilla. Fold in the sides, then roll up tightly from the bottom.
7. Wrap each burrito individually in aluminum foil.
8. Eat now, or freeze the foil-wrapped burritos in a gallon freezer bag.

> **Tip:** To reheat from frozen: remove foil, wrap in a damp paper towel, and microwave 2–2.5 minutes, flipping halfway. For crispier results, finish in a dry skillet for a minute per side.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freeze for up to 2 months. Label the freezer bag with the date — future you will appreciate it.

## Leftover Ideas
- Unwrap a burrito and chop it into a skillet for a breakfast hash
- Cut in half and serve with a side of guacamole and salsa
- Crumble into a bowl and top with sour cream for a deconstructed burrito bowl
