# Berry Protein Pancakes

> Fluffy, protein-packed pancakes that taste like a treat but won't put you back to sleep by 10 AM.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$1.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kodiak Protein Pancake Mix — 2 cups mix (~$14.99, 72 oz box)
- Kirkland Signature Frozen Mixed Berries — 1 cup (~$11.99, 4 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Greek Yogurt — 1/2 cup (~$7.99, 2-pack 48 oz tubs)
- Kirkland Signature Maple Syrup — for serving (~$14.99, 33.8 oz bottle)

## Other Ingredients
- 1.5 cups Milk (or water per box instructions)
- 2 Eggs
- 1 tablespoon Butter (for the griddle)
- 1 teaspoon Vanilla extract *(optional)*
- pinch Cinnamon *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Mix Kodiak pancake mix, milk, eggs, Greek yogurt, vanilla, and cinnamon in a large bowl. Stir until just combined — lumps are fine and actually preferred. Overmixing makes tough pancakes.
2. Gently fold in frozen berries. No need to thaw them first; they'll warm through while cooking and create little jammy pockets.
3. Heat a griddle or large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add a small pat of butter.
4. Pour about 1/3 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set — about 2–3 minutes.
5. Flip and cook 1–2 minutes more until golden brown and cooked through.
6. Serve stacked with extra berries, a dollop of Greek yogurt, and maple syrup.

> **Tip:** For meal prep, let pancakes cool completely on a wire rack, then stack with squares of parchment paper between them. Freeze in a gallon bag. Toast from frozen like a waffle — 2 minutes in the toaster and they're perfect.

## Storage
Refrigerate cooked pancakes up to 4 days. Freeze for up to 2 months. Reheat in the toaster, microwave (30 seconds), or a 350°F oven for 5 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Spread with peanut butter and roll up for a snack
- Crumble over Greek yogurt for a parfait-style breakfast
- Make mini pancake sandwiches with cream cheese and jam
