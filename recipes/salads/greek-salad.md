# Greek Salad

> Feta, olives, and the quiet confidence that comes from eating something this simple and this good.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List

- Kirkland Extra-Virgin Olive Oil — 1 bottle (~$13.99, 2 L bottle (use 1/4 cup))
- Feta Cheese Block — 1 block (~$7.99, large block (use about half))
- Roma Tomatoes — 4–5 (~$4.99, 3 lb package)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 English cucumber, chopped into chunks
- 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup Kalamata olives
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Chop the tomatoes and cucumber into generous bite-sized chunks. Nobody wants dainty pieces here — think rustic, not restaurant.
2. Thinly slice the red onion. If raw onion scares you, soak the slices in cold water for 5 minutes to mellow them out.
3. Combine the tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion in a large bowl.
4. Scatter the Kalamata olives over the top.
5. Crumble the feta generously over everything. Don't be shy — this is the main event.
6. Whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
7. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently.

> **Tip:** Let this sit 10 minutes before serving. The tomatoes release juice that becomes part of the dressing — patience pays off here.

## Storage
Keeps 2 days in the fridge. It gets a bit watery after that, but honestly still tasty. The feta holds its own.

## Leftover Ideas
- Stuff into pita bread with rotisserie chicken for quick gyros
- Toss with cooked pasta for a Greek pasta salad that travels well
- Serve over a bed of quinoa for a grain bowl that feels fancier than it is
