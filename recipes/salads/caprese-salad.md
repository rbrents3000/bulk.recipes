# Caprese Salad

> Three ingredients, zero cooking, maximum showing off — the Italian flag never tasted this good.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List

| Item | Qty | Est. Price | Package Size |
|------|-----|-----------|--------------|
| Fresh Mozzarella (Costco Deli) | 1 container | ~$7.99 | 2 x 8 oz balls |
| Roma Tomatoes | 6–8 | ~$4.99 | sold by weight |
| Fresh Basil | 1 clamshell | ~$3.49 | 4 oz package |
| Kirkland Extra-Virgin Olive Oil | 1 bottle | ~$13.99 | 2 L bottle |
| Kirkland Balsamic Vinegar | 1 bottle | ~$8.99 | 1 L bottle |

## Other Ingredients
- 1/2 teaspoon flaky sea salt (Maldon or similar)
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

## Instructions
1. Slice the mozzarella and Roma tomatoes into 1/4-inch rounds.
2. Arrange alternating slices of tomato and mozzarella on a platter, overlapping slightly. Tuck whole basil leaves between the slices.
3. Drizzle generously with extra-virgin olive oil.
4. Drizzle balsamic vinegar over the top, or serve it on the side for dipping.
5. Finish with flaky sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper.
6. Serve immediately at room temperature for the best flavor.

> **Tip:** Let the mozzarella sit at room temperature for 20 minutes before assembling. Cold mozzarella is rubbery; room-temp mozzarella is creamy and dreamy.

## Storage
Best eaten the same day. Leftovers can be refrigerated overnight in an airtight container, but the tomatoes will weep and the basil will darken. Honestly, this one's not meant to last — and it won't.

## Leftover Ideas
- Chop everything up and toss with cooked pasta for a caprese pasta salad
- Layer on crusty bread with a drizzle of olive oil for a caprese sandwich
- Dice and pile onto bruschetta for a quick appetizer
