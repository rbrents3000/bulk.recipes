# Bacon-Wrapped Stuffed Jalapenos

> Creamy cheese-stuffed jalapenos wrapped in thick-cut bacon and baked until crispy. The classic party appetizer that always gets finished first.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 12 (24 pieces) | **Cost:** ~$1.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Fresh Jalapeno Peppers — 12 large jalapenos (~$3.99, 1 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Cream Cheese — 1 bar (8 oz), softened (~$7.49, 2 x 16 oz bars)
- Kirkland Signature Thick-Sliced Bacon — 12 slices (~$18.99, 4 x 1 lb packs)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Mexican Blend Cheese — 1/2 cup (~$12.99, 2.5 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/2 teaspoon Garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon Smoked paprika
- 1/4 teaspoon Salt
- 1/4 teaspoon Black pepper
- 1 bunch, sliced Scallions *(optional, for garnish)*
- Ranch dressing, for dipping *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil and place a wire rack on top. If you don't have a rack, lay the jalapenos directly on the foil.
2. Cut each jalapeno in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scrape out the seeds and membranes. Wear gloves — jalapeno oil on your hands will burn later when you touch your face.
3. In a bowl, mix softened cream cheese, shredded cheese, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper until well combined.
4. Fill each jalapeno half generously with the cream cheese mixture, slightly mounding it.
5. Cut bacon slices in half. Wrap one half-slice of bacon tightly around each stuffed jalapeno half, starting at one end and spiraling to the other.
6. Place the wrapped jalapenos on the rack, seam-side down.
7. Bake for 25 minutes until the bacon is crispy and the filling is bubbling. For extra-crispy bacon, broil for 1-2 minutes at the end.
8. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving — the cream cheese filling is dangerously hot right out of the oven.

> **Tip:** For milder poppers, soak the seeded jalapeno halves in cold water for 30 minutes before stuffing. This pulls out a lot of the heat.

## Storage
Refrigerate for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven at 375°F for 10 minutes to re-crisp the bacon — the microwave will make it rubbery. You can also prep them fully assembled but unbaked and freeze on a sheet pan, then transfer to a freezer bag. Bake from frozen at 400°F for 30-35 minutes.

## Leftover Ideas
- Chop leftover poppers and fold into scrambled eggs or an omelet
- Dice and use as a loaded baked potato topper with sour cream
