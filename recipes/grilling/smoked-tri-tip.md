# Smoked Tri-Tip

> The cut that proves you don't need a $200 steak to feel like a pitmaster.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 45 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$5.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- USDA Choice Tri-Tip Roast — 1 roast, about 2.5 lbs (~$21.99, priced per lb)
- Kirkland Signature Black Pepper Grinder — a few generous twists (~$6.99, grinder)
- Kirkland Signature Granulated Garlic — 1 tablespoon (~$7.49, 18 oz jar)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 tablespoon Kosher salt
- 1 tablespoon Coarse black pepper
- 1 tablespoon Granulated garlic

## Instructions
1. Pull the tri-tip out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking. You want it closer to room temp so it cooks evenly.
2. Mix salt, pepper, and garlic in a small bowl. This is the holy trinity of beef rubs — don't overthink it.
3. Pat the roast dry with paper towels and season generously on all sides. Press the rub in with your hands.

### Grill Method (Indirect Heat)
4. Set up your grill for two-zone cooking — all burners on one side, none on the other. Preheat to 250°F.
5. Place the tri-tip on the cool side, fat cap up. Close the lid.
6. Cook until the internal temperature hits 125°F for medium-rare — about 35–45 minutes depending on thickness.
7. Move the tri-tip to the hot side and sear 1–2 minutes per side for a good crust.

### Smoker Method
4. Preheat your smoker to 225°F. Oak or hickory wood works beautifully here.
5. Place the tri-tip fat cap up and smoke until internal temp reaches 125°F — roughly 45–60 minutes.
6. For extra crust, finish with a hard sear on a screaming-hot grill or cast iron, 1 minute per side.

8. Rest the tri-tip on a cutting board, loosely tented with foil, for at least 10 minutes. This is not optional. Walk away, resist temptation.
9. Find the grain — it changes direction partway through the roast. Slice thin against the grain.

> **Tip:** Tri-tip has two grain directions that meet roughly in the middle. Cut the roast in half at that center point, then slice each half against its own grain. This is the difference between "wow" and "why is this chewy."

## Storage
Wrap sliced tri-tip tightly in foil or store in an airtight container. Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freezes well for up to 3 months — vacuum seal if you can.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pile onto ciabatta rolls with horseradish cream and arugula for steak sandwiches
- Slice thin and toss into a steak salad with blue cheese and balsamic
- Chop and fold into breakfast tacos with scrambled eggs and salsa
