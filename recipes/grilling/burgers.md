# Classic Smash Burgers

> The most obvious recipe we somehow didn't have yet. Fixed.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Ground Beef 80/20 — 2 lbs (~$4.99/lb, bulk pack)
- Brioche Buns or Kirkland Hamburger Buns — 8 buns (~$4.49, 8-pack)
- Kirkland Sharp Cheddar — 8 slices (~$8.99, block — slice it yourself)
- Kirkland Organic Ketchup — for serving (~$6.99, 2-pack)

## Other Ingredients
- Salt (be generous — this is a burger, not a health retreat)
- Black pepper
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- Dill pickles, sliced
- Lettuce leaves
- Tomato slices
- Onion slices (raw or grilled, your call)
- Yellow mustard

## Instructions
1. Divide the beef into 8 equal balls, about 4 oz each. Gently shape them — do NOT overwork the meat. Nobody likes a tough burger.
2. Mix Worcestershire sauce into the beef before forming balls if you want a little extra savory depth. Totally optional but recommended.
3. Heat your grill or cast iron skillet to high. You want it ripping hot. If you're using a grill, a flat cast iron griddle on the grates works beautifully.
4. Place a ball on the cooking surface and immediately smash it flat with a sturdy spatula. Press hard, press once, then leave it alone.

> **Tip:** The smash is everything. Press hard once, right when it hits the grill, then don't touch it. That crust is where the flavor lives.

5. Season the top aggressively with salt and pepper while the bottom develops a deep, dark crust — about 2–3 minutes.
6. Flip each patty. Immediately lay a slice of cheddar on top. Cook 1–2 minutes more. The cheese should be melty and slightly droopy by the time it's done.
7. While the cheese melts, toast your buns cut-side down on the grill for about 30 seconds. Watch them — they go from golden to charcoal in a blink.
8. Assemble: bottom bun, patty with cheese, pickles, onion, lettuce, tomato, ketchup, mustard, top bun. Or however you like — we're not the burger police.

> **Tip:** For extra points, spread a thin layer of mayo on the toasted bun before building. The combination of mayo, ketchup, and mustard is basically homemade special sauce.

## Sides That Go Well
- Thick-cut fries or tater tots from the Costco freezer section
- Classic coleslaw
- Corn on the cob (throw it on the grill while the burgers cook)
- Watermelon wedges if it's summer and you're feeling it

## Storage
Cooked patties keep 3–4 days in the fridge. Reheat in a skillet over medium heat — please, not the microwave. You worked too hard on that crust.

## Leftover Ideas
- Crumble leftover patties into chili or taco meat
- Chop into fried rice with soy sauce and a fried egg
- Make a chopped cheese sandwich with melted American and peppers
- Break up over nachos with all the fixings
- Dice and fold into a breakfast burrito with eggs and salsa
