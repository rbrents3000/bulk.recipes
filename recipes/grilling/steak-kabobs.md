# Steak Kabobs

> Cubed sirloin on a stick with all the veggies — grilling's greatest hits, assembled.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$5.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature USDA Choice Sirloin — 1.5 lbs, cut into 1.5-inch cubes (~$29.99, ~4 lb pack)
- Bell Peppers — 2 peppers, cut into chunks (~$6.99, 6-pack)
- Mushrooms — 8 oz whole, halved if large (~$4.49, 24 oz pack)
- Zucchini — 1 large, sliced into half-moons (~$4.99, 3-pack)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 3 tablespoons (~$13.99, 2 L)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large Red onion, cut into chunks
- 4 cloves, minced Garlic
- 1 teaspoon Salt
- 1/2 teaspoon Black pepper
- 1 teaspoon Dried oregano *(optional)*
- Metal skewers

## Instructions
1. Whisk together olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper, and oregano in a large bowl.
2. Add steak cubes and toss to coat. Let marinate 15 minutes at room temperature (or up to 2 hours in the fridge).
3. Thread steak and vegetables onto metal skewers, alternating pieces. Leave a tiny gap between each piece so everything cooks evenly.
4. Preheat grill to high heat (or set oven to broil with a rack 6 inches from the element).
5. Grill kabobs 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare, turning a quarter turn at a time. For broiling, set on a sheet pan and broil 4 minutes per side.
6. Rest kabobs 5 minutes before serving.

> **Tip:** Don't jam pieces together on the skewer — a little breathing room means even cooking and better char on the veggies.

## Storage
Refrigerate cooked kabobs in an airtight container up to 3 days. Reheat gently in a skillet to avoid overcooking the steak.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slide everything off the skewer into a warm pita with tzatziki
- Chop and toss over a big Greek salad
- Serve over rice with a drizzle of chimichurri
