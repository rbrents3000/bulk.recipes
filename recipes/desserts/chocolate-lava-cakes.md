# Chocolate Lava Cakes

> Individual molten chocolate cakes with a gooey center — easier to make than they look.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 14 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$1.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips — 1 cup (~$9.99, 4.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Unsalted Butter — 1/2 cup (1 stick) (~$4.29, 4-stick pack)
- Kirkland Large Eggs — 3 whole + 3 yolks (~$6.49, 24-count)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/3 cup All-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup Powdered sugar
- 1/2 tsp Pure vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt
- Butter and cocoa powder for greasing ramekins

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees. Butter six 6-oz ramekins generously, then dust the insides with cocoa powder, tapping out the excess.
2. Melt the chocolate chips and butter together in a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second bursts, stirring between each one. Usually takes about 90 seconds total. Let it cool slightly for 2-3 minutes.
3. Whisk in the powdered sugar until smooth, then whisk in the 3 whole eggs and 3 egg yolks one at a time. Add the vanilla and salt.
4. Fold in the flour with a spatula until just combined. Don't overmix — you want this batter to stay rich, not become cakey.
5. Divide the batter evenly among the ramekins, filling each about three-quarters full.
6. Place the ramekins on a baking sheet and bake for exactly 12-14 minutes. The edges should be set and firm, but the center should still jiggle when you gently shake the ramekin.
7. Let them cool for 1 minute — no longer or the centers will set. Run a knife around the edge of each ramekin, place a plate on top, and flip. Give it a confident tap and lift the ramekin away.
8. Serve immediately. Dust with powdered sugar or add a scoop of ice cream on the side.

> **Tip:** You can prepare the batter, fill the ramekins, and refrigerate them for up to 24 hours before baking. Add 2 extra minutes to the bake time if going straight from the fridge.

## Storage
These are best served right away — the molten center is the whole point. Leftover baked cakes can be refrigerated for 2 days and reheated in a 350-degree oven for 5-7 minutes, though they'll be more like brownies at that point.

## Leftover Ideas
- Crumble a cold one over vanilla ice cream for a brownie sundae
- Chop and fold into softened ice cream, then refreeze for a chocolate chunk ice cream
