# Berry Crumble

> Costco frozen berries baked under a buttery oat crumble — dead simple and always a crowd-pleaser.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 40 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$1.75/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Frozen Mixed Berries — 4 cups (~$11.49, 4 lb bag)
- Kirkland Unsalted Butter — 6 tbsp, cold and cubed (~$4.29, 4-stick pack)
- Kirkland Old Fashioned Rolled Oats — 1 cup (~$7.49, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Vanilla Ice Cream — for serving (~$10.49, 2-pack 1/2 gallon)

## Other Ingredients
- 3/4 cup All-purpose flour
- 2/3 cup Brown sugar, packed
- 1/4 cup Granulated sugar
- 2 tbsp Cornstarch
- 1 tsp Ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp Salt
- 1 tsp Pure vanilla extract

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees. No need to thaw the berries — frozen is actually better here because they release their juices gradually as they bake.
2. Toss the frozen berries with the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and vanilla in a large bowl. The cornstarch is what thickens the juices into a syrupy sauce instead of a watery mess. Spread the berry mixture into a 9x13-inch baking dish or a large cast iron skillet.
3. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and work it in with your fingers, pinching and squeezing until you get a shaggy, clumpy mixture with pea-sized pieces of butter still visible. Those butter chunks are what make the topping crispy.
4. Scatter the crumble topping evenly over the berries. Don't pack it down — you want it loose and craggy so it gets golden and crunchy.
5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, until the topping is deep golden brown and the berry juices are bubbling up around the edges. If the top is browning too fast, tent it loosely with foil for the last 10 minutes.
6. Let it cool for at least 15 minutes before serving. The juices need time to thicken — if you dig in too early, it'll be soupy.
7. Serve warm with a generous scoop of Kirkland vanilla ice cream on top.

> **Tip:** For extra crunch, add 1/3 cup of chopped walnuts or pecans to the crumble topping. Costco sells both in large bags that you'll use up across many recipes.

## Storage
Cover and refrigerate for up to 4 days. Reheat individual servings in a 350-degree oven for 10-12 minutes, or microwave for 60-90 seconds. The topping won't be quite as crispy after the fridge, but the oven reheat gets it close.

## Leftover Ideas
- Warm up a portion and spoon it over pancakes or waffles for breakfast
- Layer cold crumble with yogurt and granola for a quick parfait
- Fold leftover crumble into softened vanilla ice cream for a berry swirl
