# Rice Krispie Treats

> Three ingredients. Ten minutes. Zero skill required. The fact that these taste this good with this little effort is genuinely unfair to other desserts.

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 5 min | **Servings:** 24 bars | **Cost:** ~$0.25/bar

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Unsalted Butter — 3 tbsp (~$4.29, 4-stick pack)
- Large Bag of Marshmallows — 10 oz / half bag (~$5.99, full bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal
- Pinch of salt (don't skip this — it makes the sweetness pop)
- 1 tsp Vanilla extract (optional but worth it)

## Instructions
1. Melt the butter in a large pot over LOW heat.
   Low is the operative word. You want the butter barely sizzling, not browning.
2. Add the marshmallows and the vanilla extract if using.
   Stir constantly with a rubber spatula until completely melted and smooth.
   This takes about 3-4 minutes. Keep the heat low the entire time.
3. Remove the pot from heat immediately.
   This stops the cooking and keeps the marshmallow soft and stretchy.
4. Add the Rice Krispies cereal and a generous pinch of salt.
   Fold gently with the spatula until every piece of cereal is coated.
   Gentle. Like you're folding a letter, not kneading bread dough.
5. Butter a 9x13-inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper.
   Transfer the mixture to the pan.
6. Press into an even layer using buttered hands or a buttered spatula.
   Apply just enough pressure to hold the shape.
   Don't flatten them like you're angry at them. Light touch equals gooey treats.
7. Let them cool at room temperature for at least 30 minutes before cutting.
   They need time to set up but shouldn't be refrigerated.
8. Cut into 24 bars (6 rows by 4 columns).
   Try to exercise some restraint. We believe in you.

> **Tip:** The secret is LOW heat and gentle folding. If you cook the marshmallows too hot, they seize up and the treats turn hard and crunchy in all the wrong ways. Angry marshmallows = sad treats.

## Storage
Keep in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
Place wax paper or parchment between layers so they don't fuse into one mega-treat.
Do not refrigerate — cold firms up the marshmallow and kills the gooey texture.

## Leftover Ideas
- Crumble over vanilla ice cream for a ridiculous sundae
- Dip the ends in melted chocolate and let set on parchment paper
- Make s'mores treats by folding in mini chocolate chips and crushed graham crackers
- Crumble into a bowl of warm milk for the world's laziest cereal
- Press into muffin tins for individual cup-shaped treat portions
- Drizzle with peanut butter and a pinch of flaky salt
