# Bulgogi Rice Bowls

> Costco's pre-marinated bulgogi is one of the best deli items they sell. Cook it up in a hot skillet and serve over rice with quick-pickled cucumbers.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$3.67/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Korean BBQ Beef Bulgogi — 2 lbs (~$19.99, ~3 lbs)
- Kirkland Signature Jasmine Rice — 2 cups dry (4 cups cooked) (~$16.99, 25 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 1 tablespoon (~$13.99, 2 L)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large, thinly sliced Cucumber
- 3 tablespoons Rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon Sugar
- 1 tablespoon Sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon Sesame seeds
- 4 sliced Green onions
- to taste Sriracha or gochujang *(optional)*
- 1 cup Kimchi *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. Quick-pickle the cucumbers: toss sliced cucumber with rice vinegar, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Let sit while you cook the beef.
3. Heat a large skillet or wok over high heat with oil. Cook the bulgogi in batches — don't crowd the pan. You want it to sear, not steam.

> **Tip:** High heat is key. Cook in 2-3 batches so the meat gets caramelized edges instead of boiling in its own juices.

4. Sear each batch for 2-3 minutes per side until nicely browned and slightly charred.
5. Build bowls: rice on the bottom, bulgogi on top, pickled cucumbers on the side. Drizzle with sesame oil, sprinkle with sesame seeds and green onions.
6. Add sriracha, gochujang, or kimchi if you want heat.

## Storage
Refrigerate cooked bulgogi up to 4 days. Store pickled cucumbers separately for up to 5 days.

## Leftover Ideas
- Bulgogi quesadillas with mozzarella cheese
- Bulgogi fried rice the next day
- Wrap in lettuce leaves for low-carb lettuce wraps
