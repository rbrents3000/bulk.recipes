# Honey Garlic Chicken Thighs

> Costco boneless skinless chicken thighs pan-seared until golden, then glazed in a sticky honey garlic sauce. A weeknight dinner that tastes like way more effort than it is.

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs — 2.5 lbs (~$2.99/lb, sold in ~6.5 lb packs)
- Kirkland Signature Organic Honey — 1/4 cup (~$14.99, 3-pack 24 oz bottles)
- Kirkland Signature Jasmine Rice — 2 cups dry (4 cups cooked) (~$16.99, 25 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 2 tablespoons (~$13.99, 2 L)

## Other Ingredients
- 6 cloves Garlic, minced
- 3 tablespoons Soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon Rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon Sriracha *(optional, for heat)*
- 1 teaspoon Cornstarch mixed with 1 tablespoon water
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Steamed broccoli or green vegetable *(for serving)*

## Instructions
1. Start your rice according to package directions.
2. Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add the chicken thighs in a single layer — don't crowd the pan. Work in batches if needed.
4. Sear undisturbed for 5-6 minutes until the bottom is deep golden brown. Flip and cook another 5-6 minutes until cooked through (165°F internal).
5. Remove chicken to a plate and tent with foil.
6. Lower heat to medium. In the same skillet, add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
7. Add the honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sriracha. Stir and let it bubble for 1 minute.
8. Stir in the cornstarch slurry. Simmer 30 seconds until the sauce thickens and turns glossy.
9. Return the chicken to the pan and flip to coat both sides in the sauce.
10. Serve over rice with the extra sauce spooned on top.

> **Tip:** Don't move the chicken while it sears — that uninterrupted contact with the hot pan is what builds the golden crust that makes this dish.

## Storage
Refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat gently in a skillet with a splash of water to loosen the sauce, or microwave covered.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice and pile onto a baguette with pickled veggies for a banh mi-style sandwich
- Chop and stir into fried rice with a fried egg
- Dice and toss with greens for an Asian-inspired salad
