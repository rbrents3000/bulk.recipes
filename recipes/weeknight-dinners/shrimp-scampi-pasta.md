# Shrimp Scampi Pasta

> Restaurant-quality shrimp scampi using Costco's frozen shrimp and penne. Garlic, butter, white wine, lemon — done in 25 minutes.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$4.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Raw Tail-On Shrimp — 1 lb (thawed) (~$17.99, 2 lb bag, 21-25 count)
- Kirkland Signature Penne Rigate — 1 lb penne (~$8.99, 4 x 1 lb boxes)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 4 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 2 tablespoons (~$13.99, 2 L)
- Kirkland Signature Shredded Parmigiano Reggiano — 1/2 cup grated (~$14.99, 1 lb tub)
- Lemons — 2 lemons (~$4.99, 5 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 6 cloves, minced Garlic
- 1/2 cup (or sub chicken broth) Dry white wine
- 1/2 teaspoon Red pepper flakes
- 1/4 cup, chopped Fresh parsley
- to taste Salt and pepper

## Instructions
1. Cook pasta according to package directions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil and 2 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Pat shrimp dry and season with salt and pepper. Sear for 1-2 minutes per side until pink. Remove from pan.
4. In the same pan, add garlic and red pepper flakes. Cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add white wine and lemon juice. Let it reduce by half, about 2 minutes.
6. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons butter, drained pasta, and shrimp back to the pan. Toss to coat, adding pasta water a splash at a time until the sauce coats the pasta.
7. Top with Parmesan and fresh parsley. Serve immediately.

## Storage
Best eaten fresh. Leftovers keep 1-2 days refrigerated but shrimp can get rubbery when reheated.

## Leftover Ideas
- Toss cold leftovers with arugula for a pasta salad
