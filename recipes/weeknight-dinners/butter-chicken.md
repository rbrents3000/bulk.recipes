# Butter Chicken

> Tender Costco chicken thighs braised in a velvety, spiced tomato-cream sauce — the weeknight version of everyone's favorite Indian comfort food.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs — 2.5 lbs (~$8.00, 6.5 lb pack)
- Kirkland Unsalted Butter — 4 tbsp (~$0.60, 4-pack/1 lb each)
- Kirkland Organic Diced Tomatoes — 1 can, 28 oz (~$1.50, 8-pack)
- Kirkland Heavy Whipping Cream — 3/4 cup (~$1.20, 1.5 qt carton)
- Kirkland Organic Coconut Oil — 1 tbsp (~$0.15, 84 oz jar)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tbsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp sugar
- Salt to taste
- Fresh cilantro for garnish
- Basmati rice or naan for serving

## Instructions
1. Cut chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces and season generously with salt, garam masala, turmeric, cumin, paprika, and cayenne. Toss to coat evenly.
2. Melt 2 tbsp butter with the coconut oil in a large deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken in batches until golden on the outside (doesn't need to be cooked through), about 3–4 minutes per batch. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pan, melt the remaining 2 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until softened, about 4 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 30 seconds. Pour in the can of diced tomatoes with their juices. Bring to a simmer and cook for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.
5. Use an immersion blender to blend the sauce until smooth (or transfer to a blender in batches). Return to the pan if needed.
6. Stir in the heavy cream and sugar. Return the chicken pieces to the sauce. Simmer on medium-low for 8–10 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce is rich and creamy.
7. Taste and adjust salt and cayenne. Serve over basmati rice or with warm naan, garnished with fresh cilantro.

> **Tip:** Marinating the chicken in yogurt and spices for even 30 minutes makes a noticeable difference in tenderness, but it's great without it too. For a richer sauce, add an extra tablespoon of butter right at the end — that's the restaurant secret.

## Storage
Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 4 days. The sauce thickens as it cools; add a splash of water or cream when reheating. Freezes well for up to 3 months — freeze the sauce and chicken together in zip-top bags laid flat.

## Leftover Ideas
- Spoon over baked potatoes or sweet potatoes for a loaded dinner
- Use as a filling for wraps or stuffed naan with pickled onions
- Toss with cooked pasta for a fusion butter chicken pasta
