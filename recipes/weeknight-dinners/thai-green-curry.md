# Thai Green Curry

> A fragrant, coconut-milk-drenched curry that comes together faster than you can argue about what to order for dinner.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs — 1.5 lbs, sliced thin (~$5.50, 6.5 lb pack)
- Kirkland Organic Coconut Milk — 2 cans, 13.5 oz each (~$2.50, 6-pack)
- Kirkland Jasmine Rice — for serving (~$0.50, 25 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 3 tbsp green curry paste
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced thin
- 1 can (8 oz) sliced bamboo shoots, drained
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves (or regular basil)
- 1 lime, juiced
- 1 red Thai chili, sliced (optional, for heat)

## Instructions
1. Cook the jasmine rice according to package directions. A rice cooker is your best friend here — set it and forget it.
2. Heat the oil in a large deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the green curry paste and stir it around for about 1 minute until it gets fragrant and slightly darkened. This blooming step is what builds the depth of flavor.
3. Pour in one can of coconut milk (shake it first) and stir to dissolve the curry paste. Let it simmer for 2–3 minutes until the sauce starts to look glossy and slightly thickened.
4. Add the sliced chicken thighs and cook for 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is mostly cooked through.
5. Pour in the second can of coconut milk, then add the bell pepper and bamboo shoots. Stir in the fish sauce and brown sugar. Simmer for 8–10 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender.
6. Remove from heat and stir in the lime juice and Thai basil. The basil will wilt beautifully into the sauce.
7. Serve over jasmine rice, topped with sliced chili if you like it spicy.

> **Tip:** Chicken thighs are non-negotiable here — they stay juicy and tender in the simmering sauce. Chicken breast will dry out and get chalky. Trust the thigh.

## Storage
Refrigerate curry and rice separately in airtight containers for up to 4 days. The flavors actually improve overnight as everything melds together. Freezes well for up to 3 months — the coconut milk may separate slightly when thawed, but a good stir brings it back.

## Leftover Ideas
- Toss with cooked rice noodles for a curry noodle soup (add a splash of broth)
- Spoon over a baked sweet potato for a hearty lunch
- Use as a sauce for a Thai-inspired pizza with mozzarella and basil
