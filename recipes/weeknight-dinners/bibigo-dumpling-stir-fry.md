# Bibigo Dumpling Stir-Fry

> Costco's beloved Bibigo dumplings, pan-fried until crispy and tossed with vegetables in a soy-sesame sauce — a 15-minute weeknight dinner that feels like cheating.

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Bibigo Chicken & Vegetable Steamed Dumplings — 24 dumplings (~$4.00, 36 oz bag)
- Kirkland Stir-Fry Vegetable Blend (frozen) — 3 cups (~$1.50, 5.5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Organic Soy Sauce — 3 tbsp (~$0.10, 1.25 L bottle)
- Kirkland Sesame Oil — 1 tbsp (~$0.20, 12 oz bottle)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or vegetable)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp honey or brown sugar
- 1 tsp sriracha or chili garlic sauce (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
- Sliced green onions for garnish
- Sesame seeds for garnish

## Instructions
1. **Make the sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, and sriracha. Set aside.
2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large nonstick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the frozen dumplings in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until the bottoms are golden and crispy.
3. Flip the dumplings and cook for 2 more minutes. Remove from the pan and set aside.
4. Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil to the same pan. Toss in the frozen stir-fry vegetables and cook for 3–4 minutes until heated through and starting to char slightly. Add the garlic and ginger and stir for 30 seconds.
5. Pour the sauce over the vegetables. Add the cornstarch slurry and stir until the sauce thickens and coats everything, about 1 minute.
6. Return the dumplings to the pan and gently toss to coat in the sauce. Cook for 1 more minute to bring everything together.
7. Serve immediately, garnished with sliced green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Eat straight from the pan or serve over steamed rice.

> **Tip:** The key is getting a good sear on the dumplings before adding them to the stir-fry. Don't move them around — just let them sit and crisp up. If you have fresh vegetables on hand (broccoli, snap peas, mushrooms), use those instead of the frozen blend for even better texture.

## Storage
Best eaten fresh — the dumplings lose their crispiness when stored. Leftovers keep in the fridge for 1–2 days. Reheat in a hot skillet (not the microwave) to revive some of the texture.

## Leftover Ideas
- Chop leftover dumplings and fold into fried rice with an egg
- Toss with cooked ramen noodles and extra sauce for a noodle bowl
- Pack into a lunch container over rice — the sauce keeps everything moist even cold
