# Miso-Glazed Salmon

> Sweet, salty, umami-bomb salmon that caramelizes under the broiler like it went to culinary school.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 12 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$5.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Frozen Salmon Fillets — 4 fillets (~$29.99, 3 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Broccoli Florets — 2 cups (~$7.49, 4 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Jasmine Rice — 2 cups cooked (~$12.99, 25 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 3 tablespoons White miso paste
- 2 tablespoons Mirin
- 1 tablespoon Soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon Rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon Brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon Sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon, grated Fresh ginger *(optional)*
- Sesame seeds for garnish *(optional)*
- 2 Green onions, sliced *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Whisk together miso paste, mirin, soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and sesame oil until smooth.
2. Pat salmon fillets dry. Place in a shallow dish and spoon the miso glaze over each fillet, coating the top and sides.
3. If you have time, cover and marinate in the fridge for 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours). If not, proceed — it's still excellent.
4. Set oven to broil. Line a sheet pan with foil and lightly oil it.
5. Place salmon skin-side down on the pan. Broil 6 inches from the element for 8–12 minutes, until the glaze is bubbly and deeply caramelized and the fish flakes easily.
6. While salmon broils, steam broccoli and cook rice.
7. Plate salmon over rice with broccoli alongside. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions.

> **Tip:** Watch the broiler like a hawk after the 8-minute mark — miso glaze has sugar and goes from beautifully bronzed to burnt in about 30 seconds.

## Storage
Refrigerate leftover salmon up to 2 days. Best enjoyed cold or at room temperature — reheating tends to dry it out.

## Leftover Ideas
- Flake over a rice bowl with avocado, cucumber, and soy sauce
- Mix into fried rice with whatever veggies you have on hand
- Toss with cold soba noodles and a splash of sesame oil
