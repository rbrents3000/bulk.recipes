# Dump-and-Go Chili

> A no-fuss, one-pot chili where everything goes in together and simmers into a thick, hearty bowl of comfort. Ten minutes of chopping, thirty minutes of waiting.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 30 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Ground Beef 88/12 — 2 lbs (~$11.00, from 6 lb pack at ~$5.49/lb)
- Kirkland Signature Canned Black Beans — 2 cans, 15 oz each (~$1.80, 8-pack case)
- Kirkland Signature Canned Kidney Beans — 1 can, 15 oz (~$0.90, 8-pack case)
- Kirkland Signature Diced Tomatoes — 2 cans, 14.5 oz each (~$1.80, 8-pack case)
- Kirkland Signature Tomato Sauce — 1 can, 15 oz (~$0.60, 8-pack case)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tbsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp oregano
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Optional toppings: shredded cheddar, sour cream, diced avocado, tortilla chips, sliced green onions

## Instructions
1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until it starts to soften.
2. Add the ground beef and garlic. Break the meat up with a wooden spoon and cook until browned and no pink remains, about 5-6 minutes. Don't bother draining -- the fat adds flavor.
3. Add the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, cayenne, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together and cook for about 30 seconds until the spices are fragrant.
4. Dump in the diced tomatoes (with their juice), tomato sauce, drained black beans, and drained kidney beans. Stir to combine.
5. Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The longer it sits, the better the flavors meld.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning. If it needs more heat, add cayenne. If it needs brightness, stir in a squeeze of lime juice.
7. Ladle into bowls and pile on whatever toppings you like.

> **Tip:** This chili is genuinely better the next day after the flavors have had time to deepen. If you're making it for a weeknight dinner, consider cooking it the night before and reheating -- it takes almost no extra effort and the payoff is huge.

*For the Costco deli version with green peppers and a longer simmer, see the [Copycat Costco Beef Chili](../costco-copycats/deli/beef-chili.md).*

## Storage
Keeps in the fridge for up to 5 days. Reheat on the stovetop over medium-low heat or microwave for 2-3 minutes. Freezes beautifully for up to 3 months -- portion into freezer bags laid flat for easy stacking and quick thawing.

## Leftover Ideas
- Spoon over hot dogs for loaded chili dogs
- Ladle over a baked potato with cheese and sour cream
- Use as a filling for chili cheese nachos or burritos
- Stir into cooked macaroni for a quick chili mac
