# Gyro Bowls

> The Costco gyro kit gets a glow-up — bowls are just better, and you know it.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$3.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Costco Gyro Meal Kit — 1 package (~$15.99, ~2 lb kit)
- Kirkland Basmati Rice — 2 cups dry (~$0.80, from 25 lb bag)
- Kirkland Feta Cheese — 1 cup crumbled (~$2.50, from 24 oz container)
- Organic Cucumber — 1 large (~$1.00, from 3-pack)
- Red Onion — 1 medium (~$0.50, from 3 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 large tomatoes, diced
- 1 cup tzatziki (from the gyro kit or store-bought)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh dill or parsley for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Cook rice according to package directions and fluff with a fork.
2. Dice the cucumber, tomatoes, and thinly slice the red onion.
3. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with olive oil. Cook the gyro meat slices for 2-3 minutes per side until warmed through and slightly crispy on the edges.
4. Divide rice among 6 bowls. Top with gyro meat, tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and crumbled feta.
5. Drizzle tzatziki over each bowl and season with salt and pepper.

> **Tip:** Warm the pita bread from the kit in the skillet for 30 seconds per side and slice it into dipping strips on the side.

## Storage
Prep the toppings and store them separately in airtight containers for up to 4 days. Keep cooked rice and meat in their own containers. Assemble fresh each time — soggy bowls are nobody's friend.

## Leftover Ideas
- Stuff everything into a warm pita for a gyro wrap
- Toss the toppings together with leftover rice for a cold Mediterranean grain salad
- Chop up the gyro meat and scramble it into eggs for breakfast
