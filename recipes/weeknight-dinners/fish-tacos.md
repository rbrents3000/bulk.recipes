# Fish Tacos

> Crispy pan-fried fish, crunchy slaw, zippy lime crema — taco Tuesday just got a Costco-sized upgrade.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$3.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Frozen Tilapia Fillets — 4 fillets (~$17.99, 3 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Flour Tortillas — 8 tortillas (~$4.49, 2-pack of 10 count)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 3 tablespoons (~$13.99, 2 L)
- Kirkland Signature Sour Cream — 1/4 cup (~$5.99, 3 lb tub)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 teaspoon Cumin
- 1 teaspoon Chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon Garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon Salt
- 1/4 teaspoon Black pepper
- 2 cups Green cabbage, thinly shredded
- 1 Lime, juiced (plus wedges for serving)
- 2 tablespoons Mayonnaise
- to taste Fresh cilantro *(optional)*
- to taste Hot sauce *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Thaw tilapia if frozen (quickest: submerge sealed bag in cold water for 20 minutes). Pat fillets completely dry.
2. Mix cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Coat both sides of each fillet with the spice mix.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook fillets 3–4 minutes per side until golden and flaky. Break into large chunks.
4. Make the lime crema: stir together sour cream, mayo, and half the lime juice. Season with a pinch of salt.
5. Toss shredded cabbage with remaining lime juice and a pinch of salt.
6. Warm tortillas in a dry skillet or directly over a gas flame for a few seconds per side.
7. Build tacos: tortilla, cabbage slaw, fish chunks, drizzle of lime crema, cilantro, and hot sauce.

> **Tip:** You can swap tilapia for Costco's frozen mahi-mahi fillets for a meatier, slightly sweeter taco — both work great.

## Storage
Store cooked fish and slaw separately in the fridge up to 2 days. Assemble tacos fresh for the best texture.

## Leftover Ideas
- Make a fish taco bowl over rice with all the toppings
- Flake leftover fish into a quesadilla with cheese and salsa
- Toss fish chunks into a salad with the lime crema as dressing
