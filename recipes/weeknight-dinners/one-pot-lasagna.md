# One-Pot Lasagna

> All the lasagna vibes with none of the architectural ambition.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Ground Beef (85/15) — 1.5 lbs (~$5.50, from 4 lb pack)
- Rao's Marinara Sauce — 1 jar (24 oz) (~$7.00, from 2-pack)
- Kirkland Shredded Mozzarella — 2 cups (~$2.50, from 2.5 lb bag)
- Galbani Ricotta Cheese — 1 cup (~$2.50, from 32 oz container)

## Other Ingredients
- 8 oz lasagna noodles, broken into rough pieces
- 3 cups water
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
- Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Brown the ground beef in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, breaking it into crumbles. Drain any excess fat.
2. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Pour in the marinara sauce and water. Stir in Italian seasoning, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
4. Add the broken lasagna noodles, pushing them down into the liquid. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer for 15-18 minutes, stirring occasionally, until noodles are tender.
5. Drop spoonfuls of ricotta across the top. Sprinkle mozzarella over everything. Cover and cook 3-4 more minutes until the cheese is melted and gooey.
6. Let it rest for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil if you're feeling fancy.

> **Tip:** Resist the urge to stir after adding the cheeses — you want those pockets of ricotta, not a uniform cheese soup.

## Storage
Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 4 days. This actually tastes even better the next day after the noodles have soaked up more sauce. Reheat on the stovetop with a splash of water to loosen things up.

## Leftover Ideas
- Scoop it into a baked potato for a carb-on-carb masterpiece
- Spread into a baking dish, top with extra mozzarella, and broil for a crispy-topped leftover bake
- Stuff into bell peppers and bake at 375F for 20 minutes
