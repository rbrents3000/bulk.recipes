# Seared Scallops

> Restaurant-quality scallops at home — golden crust, buttery center, and you didn't even need a reservation.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 8 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$8.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Wild Sea Scallops — 1 lb (~$24.99, 2 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Unsalted Butter — 3 tablespoons (~$12.99, 4 x 1 lb bars)
- Lemons — 1 lemon (~$4.99, 5 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 tablespoon Olive oil or avocado oil (high smoke point)
- 3 cloves, minced Garlic
- 1/4 cup Dry white wine
- 1/2 teaspoon Salt
- 1/4 teaspoon Black pepper
- 2 tablespoons, chopped Fresh parsley *(optional)*
- Pasta or crusty bread for serving

## Instructions
1. Pat scallops extremely dry with paper towels — this is the single most important step. Wet scallops steam instead of sear. Remove the small side muscle if still attached.
2. Season scallops with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. Heat oil in a large stainless steel or cast iron skillet over high heat until the oil just begins to smoke.
4. Place scallops in the pan one at a time, leaving at least an inch between each. Do not move them. Sear 2–3 minutes until a deep golden crust forms on the bottom.
5. Flip each scallop. Add butter and garlic to the pan. Cook 1–2 minutes more, spooning the melted butter over the scallops as they finish.
6. Remove scallops to a plate. Add wine and lemon juice to the pan, scraping up the browned bits. Let it reduce for 1 minute into a quick pan sauce.
7. Drizzle sauce over scallops. Garnish with parsley. Serve immediately with pasta or crusty bread.

> **Tip:** Do not crowd the pan — cook in two batches if needed. A crowded pan drops the temperature and you'll get sad, gray, steamed scallops instead of a gorgeous sear.

## Storage
Scallops are best eaten immediately. Leftovers can be refrigerated up to 1 day — eat cold over a salad rather than reheating.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice and serve cold over arugula with lemon vinaigrette
- Chop and fold into risotto or pasta with cream sauce
- Dice into a quick scallop ceviche with lime, cilantro, and avocado
