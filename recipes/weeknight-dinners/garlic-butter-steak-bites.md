# Garlic Butter Steak Bites

> Tender cubes of Costco sirloin seared in a rich garlic butter, paired with crispy roasted potatoes for a steakhouse dinner in under 30 minutes.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$5.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- USDA Choice Beef Top Sirloin — 1.5 lbs, cut into 1-inch cubes (~$15.99, 3 lb pack)
- Kirkland Signature Butter — 3 tablespoons (~$12.49, 4-pack/1 lb each)
- Gold Potatoes — 1.5 lbs, cut into 3/4-inch cubes (~$7.99, 5 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Extra Virgin Olive Oil — 2 tablespoons (~$13.99, 2 L)

## Other Ingredients
- 6 cloves, minced Garlic
- 1 teaspoon Kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon Black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon Smoked paprika
- 2 sprigs Fresh thyme *(or 1/2 teaspoon dried)*
- 1 tablespoon Fresh parsley, chopped *(for garnish)*

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss potato cubes with olive oil, salt, and smoked paprika on a sheet pan. Roast for 20 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and crispy.
2. While potatoes roast, pat steak cubes dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Heat a large cast-iron or heavy skillet over high heat. Add 1 tablespoon butter and sear steak bites in a single layer, about 2 minutes per side for medium. Work in batches to avoid crowding the pan.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining 2 tablespoons butter, minced garlic, and thyme to the skillet. Baste the steak bites with the melted garlic butter for about 1 minute until fragrant.
5. Plate the steak bites over roasted potatoes. Spoon any remaining garlic butter from the pan on top and finish with chopped parsley.

> **Tip:** Let the skillet get screaming hot before adding the steak. A proper sear means better flavor and no gray, steamed meat. Don't move the cubes until they release easily from the pan.

## Storage
Refrigerate steak and potatoes separately for up to 3 days. Reheat in a skillet over medium heat to keep the steak from overcooking.

## Leftover Ideas
- Slice steak bites thin and pile into warm tortillas with chimichurri for steak tacos
- Chop and fold into a breakfast hash with eggs and leftover potatoes
