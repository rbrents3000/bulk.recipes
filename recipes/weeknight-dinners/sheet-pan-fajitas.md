# Sheet Pan Chicken Fajitas

> One pan, zero negotiation about who's doing the dishes.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.75/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs — 2 lbs (~$5.00, from 6.5 lb pack)
- Organic Bell Peppers — 3 (mix of colors) (~$3.00, from 6-pack)
- Flour Tortillas — 12 (~$3.50, from 2-pack of 10)
- Kirkland Olive Oil — 3 tbsp (~$0.30, from 2L bottle)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large yellow onion, sliced
- 2 limes, juiced
- 1 tbsp cumin
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- Optional toppings: sour cream, guacamole, shredded cheese, salsa

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425F. Line a large sheet pan with foil or parchment paper.
2. Slice chicken thighs into thin strips. Slice bell peppers and onion into similar-sized strips.
3. Toss chicken, peppers, and onion on the sheet pan with olive oil, cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread in a single layer (use two pans if needed — crowding is the enemy of crispy edges).
4. Roast for 20 minutes, tossing halfway through, until chicken is cooked through and vegetables have some nice charred spots.
5. Squeeze fresh lime juice over everything. Serve in warm tortillas with your favorite toppings.

> **Tip:** Broil for the last 2 minutes to get those blackened, slightly blistered edges that make fajitas taste like a restaurant made them.

## Storage
Store cooked fajita filling in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat in a hot skillet to bring back the texture — microwaving works but won't give you that sizzle.

## Leftover Ideas
- Layer into a quesadilla with extra cheese
- Top a salad for a Tex-Mex grain bowl
- Scramble with eggs and roll into a breakfast burrito
