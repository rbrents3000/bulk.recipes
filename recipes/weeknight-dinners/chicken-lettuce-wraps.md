# Chicken Lettuce Wraps

> PF Chang's called — they want their recipe back. Too bad, we're making it at home with Costco chicken for a fraction of the price.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Ground Chicken — 1.5 lbs (~$5.00, 4 lb pack)
- Kirkland Organic Soy Sauce — 3 tbsp (~$0.20, 1.2 L bottle)
- Kirkland Organic Extra Virgin Olive Oil — for drizzling (~$0.10, 2 L bottle)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 can (8 oz) water chestnuts, drained and finely diced
- 2 tbsp hoisin sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp sriracha (adjust to taste)
- 3 green onions, thinly sliced
- 1 head butter lettuce, leaves separated into cups
- Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

## Instructions
1. Separate the butter lettuce leaves and set them out on a platter. You want the cup-shaped inner leaves — they're nature's perfect taco shell.
2. Mix the soy sauce, hoisin, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and sriracha in a small bowl. Set this sauce aside.
3. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat with a drizzle of olive oil. Add the ground chicken and break it up with a spatula into small crumbles. Cook for 5–6 minutes until browned and no longer pink.
4. Add the garlic and ginger, stirring constantly for about 30 seconds until the kitchen smells incredible.
5. Toss in the diced water chestnuts and pour the sauce over everything. Stir well and cook for another 2–3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and coats the chicken.
6. Remove from heat and stir in most of the green onions, saving some for garnish.
7. Spoon the chicken mixture into the butter lettuce cups. Top with remaining green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds.

> **Tip:** The key to great lettuce wraps is getting the chicken nice and browned — don't stir it constantly. Let it sit in the pan and develop some color before breaking it up. That browning is flavor.

## Storage
Store the chicken filling in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. Keep the lettuce cups separate and unwashed until ready to serve. Reheat the filling in a skillet with a tiny splash of water.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pile the filling into a rice bowl with pickled veggies and a fried egg
- Stuff it into warm tortillas with shredded cabbage for fusion tacos
- Toss with cooked ramen noodles and a drizzle of sesame oil
