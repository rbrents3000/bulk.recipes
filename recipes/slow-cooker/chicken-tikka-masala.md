# Chicken Tikka Masala

> Restaurant-quality tikka masala that simmers itself while you binge-watch your shows.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 6 hr | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$2.75/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Chicken Thighs — 2.5 lbs, boneless skinless (~$14.99, 6.5 lb pack)
- Kirkland Heavy Cream — 1 cup (~$9.99, half gallon)
- Kirkland Jasmine Rice — for serving (~$16.99, 25 lb bag)
- Kirkland Diced Tomatoes — 1 can (28 oz) (~$6.49, 8-pack cans)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tbsp garam masala
- 1 tbsp cumin
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- Salt to taste
- Fresh cilantro for garnish

## Instructions
1. Place chicken thighs in the slow cooker.
2. In a bowl, combine diced tomatoes, tomato paste, onion, garlic, ginger, garam masala, cumin, turmeric, paprika, cayenne, and salt. Pour over chicken.
3. Cover and cook on LOW for 6 hours or HIGH for 3 hours.
4. Remove chicken and shred or cube it, then return to the sauce.
5. Stir in heavy cream and let it warm through for 10-15 minutes on HIGH.
6. Cook jasmine rice according to package directions.
7. Serve tikka masala over rice, garnished with fresh cilantro.

> **Tip:** That 25 lb bag of Kirkland jasmine rice costs about what you'd pay for 5 lbs at a regular store. You'll use it for everything. No regrets.

## Storage
Refrigerate for up to 4 days. Freeze sauce and chicken together for up to 3 months. The flavors actually get better overnight, so day-two tikka masala is a feature, not a bug.

## Leftover Ideas
- Stuff into naan or tortillas for tikka masala wraps
- Spoon over baked potatoes for a fusion comfort meal
- Toss with cooked pasta for a creamy tikka masala pasta
