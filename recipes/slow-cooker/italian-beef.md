# Chicago-Style Italian Beef

> A sandwich so good that people drive across state lines for it. The slow cooker handles the 8-hour braise while you handle literally anything else.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 8 hr (low) | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- USDA Choice Beef Chuck Roast — 3 lbs (~$6.99/lb, sold per lb)
- Kirkland Chicken Broth — 2 cups (~$4.99, 6-pack cartons)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 packet Italian dressing mix
  (or 2 tbsp Italian seasoning + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp onion powder)
- 1 jar Giardiniera, hot or mild, 16 oz — this is non-negotiable
- 4-5 Pepperoncini peppers + 1/4 cup of their jar juice
- 8 Hoagie rolls (sturdy ones — they need to survive the jus)

## Instructions
1. Place the chuck roast in the slow cooker fat-side up.
   Don't trim the fat — it renders down and creates incredible braising liquid.
2. Sprinkle the Italian dressing mix all over the top and sides of the roast.
   If using homemade seasoning, combine it first, then sprinkle.
3. Tuck the pepperoncini peppers around the roast.
   Pour in their juice along with the chicken broth.
4. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours.
   Do not open the lid to check. Trust is important in every relationship, including the one with your slow cooker.
5. The meat should shred with zero resistance.
   If it fights back at all, give it another hour.
6. Pull the beef out onto a cutting board.
   Shred with two forks, discarding any large fat chunks.
7. Return the shredded beef to the pot.
   Let it swim in the jus for 10 minutes — this is the soak-up step and it matters.
8. Toast the hoagie rolls under the broiler until just golden, about 2 minutes.
   Watch them like a hawk. Broilers go from golden to charcoal in seconds.
9. Pile the beef generously onto the rolls.
   Spoon jus directly over the meat. Top with a heavy hand of giardiniera.
10. Serve each sandwich with a small bowl of warm jus for dipping.
    Dunk the whole sandwich. This is the way.

> **Tip:** The giardiniera is what makes this Chicago-style — spicy pickled vegetables with heat, acid, and crunch against the tender beef. Don't skip it. If you can find Marconi brand, that's the gold standard.

## Storage
Beef and jus refrigerate together for up to 5 days.
Freezes well for up to 3 months.
The jus solidifies into a beefy gelatin in the fridge — that's a sign of quality, not a problem.
Reheat on the stove over medium-low until liquid and warmed through.

## Leftover Ideas
- Spread on pizza dough with mozzarella for Italian beef pizza
- Stuff into quesadillas with provolone cheese
- Toss into fried rice with soy sauce and sesame oil (seriously, it works)
- Serve over creamy polenta with a ladle of jus
- Pile onto nachos with melted provolone and pickled peppers
- Wrap in egg roll wrappers and fry for Italian beef egg rolls
