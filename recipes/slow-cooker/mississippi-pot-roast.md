# Mississippi Pot Roast

> Five ingredients. Zero cooking rules followed. Unreasonably delicious.

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 8 hr | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$3.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Chuck Roast — 3-4 lbs (~$22.00, sold per lb)
- Pepperoncini Peppers — 8-10 whole peppers + a splash of jar juice (~$3.99, jar)
- Butter — 1/2 stick (~$8.99, Kirkland 4-pack, 1 lb each)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 packet ranch seasoning mix (1 oz)
- 1 packet au jus gravy mix (1 oz)

## Instructions
1. Place chuck roast in the slow cooker.
2. Sprinkle ranch seasoning and au jus mix over the top.
3. Scatter pepperoncini peppers around and over the roast. Add a splash of pepperoncini juice.
4. Place the half stick of butter on top of the roast.
5. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours or HIGH for 4-5 hours.
6. Shred with two forks. Stir the meat into the sauce.
7. Try not to eat the entire batch standing over the slow cooker.

> **Tip:** This recipe has no business being this good with so few ingredients. Don't question it. Just trust the process.

## Storage
Refrigerate for up to 4 days. Freezes well for up to 3 months. The fat will solidify on top in the fridge -- just reheat and it melts right back into saucy perfection.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pile onto hoagie rolls for Mississippi pot roast sandwiches
- Serve over mashed potatoes or egg noodles
- Load onto nachos with cheese and pickled jalapenos
- Stuff into quesadillas with shredded cheese
