# Slow Cooker French Dip Sandwiches

> Set it, forget it, and come home to the most ridiculously dippable sandwich of your life.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 8 hr | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Chuck Roast — 3 lbs (~$22.00, sold per lb at ~$6.99/lb)
- Kirkland Beef Broth — 4 cups (~$11.49, 6-pack cartons)
- Kirkland Provolone Cheese — 8 slices (~$8.99, 2 lb pack)
- Hoagie/Sub Rolls — 8 rolls (~$4.99, bakery pack)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 large yellow onion, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

## Instructions
1. Place the sliced onion in an even layer on the bottom of the slow cooker. This is your roast's little bed — treat it right.
2. Set the chuck roast on top of the onions.
3. Pour beef broth over the roast, then add Worcestershire sauce, garlic, Italian seasoning, thyme, salt, and pepper.
4. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours, until the beef is fall-apart tender and practically shreds itself.
5. Remove the roast and shred with two forks. It should put up absolutely no resistance.
6. Return the shredded beef to the slow cooker and stir it into the cooking liquid. Let it soak for a few minutes — this is where the magic happens.

> **Tip:** Don't skip the soaking step. That beefy, onion-y broth is the whole point. Your meat needs a bath.

7. Scoop shredded beef onto hoagie rolls and top each with a slice of provolone.
8. Broil open-faced in the oven for 1-2 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
9. Ladle the remaining cooking liquid through a fine mesh strainer into small bowls for dipping — that's your au jus, and it's liquid gold.

> **Tip:** If you want to go full Costco power move, grab their French onion soup mix and add a packet to the broth before cooking. It's borderline cheating, but nobody's keeping score.

## Storage
Refrigerate shredded beef and au jus separately in airtight containers for up to 4 days. Freezes well for up to 3 months. The fat will solidify on top of the au jus in the fridge — just reheat and it melts right back into beefy goodness.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pile the beef onto a baguette with Swiss cheese for a next-day French dip remix
- Toss with egg noodles and a ladle of au jus for a lazy beef stroganoff situation
- Load onto nachos with melted provolone and pickled onions
- Stuff into flour tortillas with cheese for French dip quesadillas — trust us on this one
