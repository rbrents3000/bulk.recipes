# Honey Garlic Pork

> Five ingredients. One slow cooker. Eight hours of doing absolutely nothing. Dinner.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 8 hr (low) or 4 hr (high) | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$2.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Pork Shoulder/Butt — 3-4 lbs (~$2.49/lb, sold per lb)
- Kirkland Honey — 1/3 cup (~$12.99, 5 lb jug)

## Other Ingredients
- 1/3 cup Soy sauce
- 4 cloves Garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp Ketchup
- 1 tbsp Sriracha or red chili flakes (optional, but encouraged)
- 1 tbsp Cornstarch
- 2 tbsp Cold water

## Instructions
1. Place the pork shoulder in the slow cooker fat-side up.
   No searing, no seasoning, no drama. Just set it in there.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, ketchup, and sriracha.
   Pour the mixture evenly over the pork.
3. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours or HIGH for 4 hours.
   The pork should be fall-apart tender — if you can shred it with a disappointed glance, it's ready.
   If it resists the fork at all, give it another 30 minutes.
4. Carefully remove the pork to a cutting board.
   Shred it with two forks, discarding any large pieces of fat.
5. Return the shredded pork to the slow cooker.
   Stir it into the cooking liquid so every shred is coated.
6. In a small cup, mix the cornstarch and cold water into a smooth slurry.
   Stir it into the liquid around the pork.
7. Cook on HIGH with the lid off for 15 more minutes.
   The sauce should become glossy, thick, and coat the back of a spoon.
8. Give it one final stir and let it sit with the lid on for 10 minutes.
   This is the patience step. The pork absorbs the sauce. Don't skip it.

> **Tip:** The shred-and-return move is key — that rest in the sauce is when it goes from "good pork" to "where has this been all my life." Want crispy edges? Spread some on a sheet pan and broil for 3-4 minutes.

## Storage
Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 5 days.
Freezes well for up to 3 months — freeze in individual portions for easy weeknight dinners.
The sauce thickens in the fridge, which is normal.
Add a splash of water when reheating on the stove or in the microwave.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pile onto slider buns with a quick pickle slaw
- Serve over jasmine rice with a fried egg for lazy rice bowls
- Toss into fried rice with frozen veggies and scrambled eggs
- Wrap in butter lettuce for low-carb lettuce wraps
- Drop into a bowl of instant ramen for an instant upgrade
- Fill flour tortillas with pork, pickled onions, and cilantro
- Stir into mac and cheese for a sweet-savory situation
