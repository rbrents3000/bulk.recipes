# Beef Stew

> The slow cooker does all the work while you pretend to be productive.

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 8 hr | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$3.00/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Chuck Roast — 3 lbs, cubed (~$22.00, sold per lb)
- Yukon Gold Potatoes — 5 medium (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)
- Carrots — 6 large, peeled and chunked (~$4.99, 10 lb bag)
- Yellow Onions — 2 large, diced (~$3.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Chicken Broth — 4 cups (~$11.49, 6-pack cartons)
- Kirkland Tomato Paste — 3 tbsp (~$7.49, 12-pack cans)

## Other Ingredients
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tbsp flour
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Toss chuck roast cubes with flour, salt, and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over high heat and sear beef in batches until browned on all sides. Transfer to slow cooker.
3. Add potatoes, carrots, onions, and garlic to the slow cooker.
4. Pour in chicken broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, and rosemary. Stir to combine.
5. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours or HIGH for 4 hours, until beef is fork-tender and vegetables are soft.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning before serving.

> **Tip:** Searing the beef first isn't strictly required, but it builds a deeper flavor that's absolutely worth the extra 10 minutes. Your house will smell incredible — that alone is reason enough.

## Storage
Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Reheat on the stove over medium-low, adding a splash of broth if it's thickened up.

## Leftover Ideas
- Ladle over egg noodles or mashed potatoes for a next-day comfort meal
- Spoon into bread bowls for an impressive-looking lunch with zero extra effort
- Toss with cooked pappardelle pasta and a sprinkle of parmesan
