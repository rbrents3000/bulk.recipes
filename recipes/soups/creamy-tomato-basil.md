# Creamy Tomato Basil Soup

> The grilled cheese's best friend — silky, bright, and absolutely begging to be dunked into.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 6 | **Cost:** ~$1.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List

- Kirkland Canned Diced Tomatoes — 2 cans (~$7.49, 8-pack / 14.5 oz each)
- Kirkland Chicken Broth — 1 carton (~$4.49, 6-pack / 32 oz each)
- Kirkland Heavy Cream — 1 carton (~$4.99, 1 qt)
- Fresh Basil — 1 clamshell (~$3.49, 4 oz package)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 teaspoon sugar (cuts the acidity)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

## Instructions
1. Heat olive oil and butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened and translucent.
2. Add garlic and red pepper flakes, stirring for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in both cans of diced tomatoes (with their juice) and 2 cups chicken broth. Add sugar, salt, and pepper.
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 15 minutes.
5. Remove from heat. Add a generous handful of fresh basil leaves (about 1/2 cup packed).
6. Blend until smooth using an immersion blender or regular blender (vent the lid!).
7. Return to low heat. Stir in 3/4 cup heavy cream and warm through for 2–3 minutes.
8. Taste, adjust seasoning, and serve with a few torn basil leaves on top.

> **Tip:** Yes, you should make grilled cheese to go with this. Use the Kirkland sliced cheddar — it melts like a dream and you already have it in the cart.

*For the sweeter, creamier Costco deli version, see the [Copycat Costco Tomato Basil Soup](../costco-copycats/deli/tomato-basil-soup.md).*

## Storage
Refrigerate for up to 5 days. Freezes for up to 3 months — add the cream after thawing and reheating for the best texture.

## Leftover Ideas
- Use as a pasta sauce — toss with penne and parmesan
- Pour over meatballs for a quick sub filling
- Thin with a little broth and use as a shakshuka base for poaching eggs
