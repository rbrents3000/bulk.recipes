# Corn Chowder

> Sweet, creamy, and smoky. The soup that makes you wonder why you ever bothered with cream of anything from a can.

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 25 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Signature Thick-Cut Bacon — 6 strips (~$14.99, 4-pack)
- Russet Potatoes — 2 large, peeled and cubed (~$5.99, 10 lb bag)
- Kirkland Signature Chicken Broth — 3 cups (~$2.00, from 6-pack of 32 oz cartons)
- Kirkland Heavy Whipping Cream — 1 cup (~$11.99, half gallon)

## Other Ingredients
- 4 cups corn kernels (fresh off the cob in summer, or frozen — no shame in it)
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp butter
- 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper *(optional, for a warm kick)*
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh chives, chopped, for topping
- Good olive oil for drizzling *(optional)*

## Instructions
1. Cook the bacon in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat until perfectly crispy and golden. Remove, crumble onto a plate, and set aside for topping.
2. Leave 1-2 tablespoons of bacon fat in the pot — this is the flavor foundation and you will not apologize for it.
3. Add butter to the bacon fat and melt over medium heat. Sauté the onion for 4-5 minutes until soft and translucent.
4. Add garlic, smoked paprika, and cayenne if using. Stir for 30 seconds until everything smells incredible.
5. Sprinkle in the flour and stir constantly for 1 minute to make a quick roux. Slowly pour in the chicken broth while whisking to prevent any lumps.
6. Add the cubed potatoes, corn kernels, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
7. Cook for 20 minutes until the potatoes are completely tender when pierced with a fork.
8. Remove the bay leaf. Using an immersion blender, blend about half the soup right in the pot until creamy but still pleasantly chunky. No immersion blender? Carefully scoop half into a regular blender (vent the lid — hot soup explodes), blend smooth, and pour it back.
9. Stir in the heavy cream and heat through for 2-3 minutes over medium-low. Don't let it boil — simmering cream is happy cream, boiling cream is broken cream.
10. Season generously with salt and pepper. Corn is sweet, so it can handle bold seasoning. Taste and adjust.
11. Ladle into bowls and pile on the crumbled bacon, fresh chives, and an extra grind of black pepper. A drizzle of good olive oil on top doesn't hurt either.

> **Tip:** If you can get fresh corn in summer, char the ears on the grill or in a screaming-hot cast iron skillet before cutting the kernels off. The smoky sweetness takes this from good to absolutely unreal.

> **Tip:** After cutting corn off the cob, scrape the cob with the back of your knife to extract the milky starch. Stir that into the soup — it adds natural body and sweetness you can't get any other way.

## Storage
Refrigerate for up to 5 days in an airtight container. Reheat gently on the stovetop over medium-low heat — the cream can separate if you blast it in the microwave. Give it a good stir and it'll come right back together. Freezes well for up to 3 months.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour over a split baked potato and top with cheddar for a chowder-loaded potato
- Stir in shredded rotisserie chicken and a handful of black beans for a heartier Southwestern chowder
- Mix with cooked pasta shells, pour into a baking dish, top with cheddar and breadcrumbs, and broil for a corn chowder pasta bake
- Serve chilled on a hot summer day — cold corn chowder is a seriously underrated move
