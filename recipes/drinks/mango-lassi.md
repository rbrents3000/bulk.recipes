# Mango Lassi

> A refreshing Indian yogurt drink that turns frozen mango chunks into liquid sunshine.

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$1.25/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Frozen Mango Chunks — 2 cups (~$11.99, 4 lb bag)
- Kirkland Greek Yogurt — 1 cup (~$7.99, 2-pack tubs)
- Kirkland Honey — 2 tbsp (~$12.99, 5 lb jug)

## Other Ingredients
- 1 cup milk (whole milk is traditional, any kind works)
- 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
- Pinch of saffron threads (optional, for the fancy version)

## Instructions
1. Add frozen mango chunks, Greek yogurt, milk, honey, and cardamom to a blender.
2. Blend on high until smooth and frothy, about 60 seconds.
3. Taste and adjust sweetness -- ripe mangoes may need less honey.
4. Pour into glasses. If feeling traditional, sprinkle a tiny pinch of cardamom on top.
5. Close your eyes, take a sip, and pretend you're somewhere tropical.

> **Tip:** Cardamom is the star here -- it's what makes this taste like an authentic lassi instead of just a mango smoothie. A little goes a long way, so start with 1/4 tsp and work up.

## Storage
Best served fresh. Can be refrigerated for up to 24 hours in a sealed jar. Stir well before drinking as the mango tends to settle.

## Leftover Ideas
- Freeze into popsicle molds for mango lassi pops
- Pour over chia seeds and refrigerate overnight for mango chia pudding
- Use as a dipping sauce base for fruit skewers at a party
