# Berry Smoothie

> Breakfast in a glass for people who think they don't have time for breakfast.

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 4 | **Cost:** ~$1.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Frozen Three Berry Blend — 3 cups (~$11.99, 4 lb bag)
- Kirkland Greek Yogurt — 1 cup (~$7.99, 2-pack tubs)
- Kirkland Honey — 2 tbsp (~$12.99, 5 lb jug)

## Other Ingredients
- 2 bananas
- 1 cup milk (any kind)
- Ice cubes (optional, for extra thickness)

## Instructions
1. Add frozen berries, Greek yogurt, bananas, honey, and milk to a blender.
2. Blend on high for 60 seconds until smooth and creamy.
3. If it's too thick, add a splash more milk. Too thin, add a handful of ice or more frozen berries.
4. Pour into glasses and serve immediately.
5. Feel virtuous about your life choices for at least 20 minutes.

> **Tip:** That 4 lb bag of frozen berries will make this recipe multiple times. Keep bananas in the freezer too -- peel them first and freeze on a tray, then toss into a zip-lock bag. Frozen bananas make smoothies extra thick and creamy.

## Storage
Best served immediately. If you must store it, pour into a jar and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Give it a vigorous shake before drinking -- it will separate, and that's normal.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour into popsicle molds for frozen berry pops
- Use as a base for smoothie bowls topped with granola and fresh fruit
- Freeze in ice cube trays and blend again later for an instant smoothie
