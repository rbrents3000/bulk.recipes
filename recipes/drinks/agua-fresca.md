# Agua Fresca

> Mexico's answer to "I have too much fruit" — and honestly, it's the best answer.

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 0 min | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$0.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Frozen Mango Chunks — about 1 lb (~$9.99, 3 lb bag) **OR** Whole Watermelon — use half (~$6.99)
- Limes — juice of 2 (~$4.99, 3 lb bag)

## Other Ingredients
- 6 cups cold water
- 1/4 cup sugar or honey (adjust to sweetness of your fruit)
- Ice for serving
- Fresh mint leaves (optional, but they take it from good to gorgeous)
- Pinch of salt (optional — same trick as margarita salt, brings out the fruit flavor)

## Instructions
1. If using frozen mango, let it thaw on the counter for about 10 minutes so your blender doesn't sound like it's grinding rocks.
2. If using watermelon, cut it into chunks and remove any seeds you see. Don't stress about the small white ones.
3. Add the fruit and 2 cups of water to a blender. Blend on high until completely smooth, about 30-45 seconds.
4. Here's where the two paths diverge:
   - **Mango:** Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a large pitcher. Use a spoon to press the pulp and get all the liquid through. Discard the fibrous bits.
   - **Watermelon:** Pour straight into the pitcher — no straining needed. It blends silky smooth on its own.
5. Add the remaining 4 cups of cold water to the pitcher.
6. Squeeze in the juice of 2 limes.
7. Add the sugar or honey and stir until dissolved. Start with less — you can always add more.
8. Taste and adjust. More lime if it's too sweet, more sweetener if the fruit wasn't at peak ripeness.
9. Add a pinch of salt if you want. It sounds weird but it makes the fruit flavor pop.
10. Serve over plenty of ice, garnished with mint leaves and a lime wheel if you're showing off.
11. For a spicy kick, add a few thin slices of jalapeño to the pitcher and let them steep for 15-20 minutes before serving. Remove them before your guests arrive unless you want to see some interesting facial expressions.
12. If serving at a party, set out the pitcher with a ladle or pour it into a dispenser with a spigot. It looks beautiful and disappears fast.

> **Tip:** Watermelon agua fresca barely needs any added sugar — taste it before you sweeten. Mango usually wants the full 1/4 cup. And if you have both fruits on hand, mixing them together is absolutely not against the rules.

> **Tip:** For a party, make a double batch and serve it from a big glass dispenser with lime wheels and mint floating in it. It looks like a Pinterest photo and costs about $4 total.

## Variations
- **Strawberry:** Use 1 lb fresh or frozen strawberries. Strain the seeds out unless you don't mind them.
- **Cucumber-Lime:** Blend 2 cucumbers (peeled) with lime juice and a touch of sugar. Incredibly refreshing on a hot day.
- **Pineapple:** Use 2 cups fresh or canned pineapple chunks. Strain the pulp for a cleaner drink.

## Storage
Keeps 3-4 days in the fridge in a sealed pitcher. The fruit solids will settle to the bottom — just give it a good stir before each pour. The watermelon version may get a little foamy on top after sitting; that's totally normal, stir it right in.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour into popsicle molds for homemade paletas
- Use as a cocktail mixer — mango agua fresca with tequila and a tajin rim is a life-changing decision
- Pour over shaved ice for an instant raspado
- Freeze in ice cube trays and add to plain water throughout the week
- Blend with ice for a slushy version on hot days
