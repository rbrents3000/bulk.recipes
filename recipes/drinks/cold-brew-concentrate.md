# Cold Brew Concentrate

> One batch. One week of coffee. Zero mornings spent waiting in a drive-through.

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 0 min (12-24 hr steep) | **Servings:** 8 | **Cost:** ~$0.50/serving

## Costco Shopping List
- Kirkland Colombian Coffee — 1 cup, coarsely ground (~$14.99, 3 lb can)

## Other Ingredients
- 4 cups cold filtered water
- Milk, cream, or sweetener of choice for serving

## Instructions
1. Add coarsely ground coffee to a large jar, pitcher, or French press.
2. Pour in cold filtered water. Stir gently to make sure all grounds are saturated.
3. Cover and place in the refrigerator.
4. Steep for 12-24 hours. Longer = stronger, but anything over 24 hours can get bitter.
5. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve lined with cheesecloth, a coffee filter, or just use the French press plunger.
6. Transfer the concentrate to a clean jar or bottle.
7. To serve, dilute 1:1 with cold water or milk over ice. Adjust the ratio to your caffeine personality.

> **Tip:** That 3 lb can of Kirkland coffee will make roughly 12 batches of cold brew concentrate. At $0.50 a serving, your coffee shop habit just got very hard to justify.

## Storage
Refrigerate the concentrate in a sealed container for up to 2 weeks. It actually mellows and smooths out over the first few days. Label it so nobody mistakes it for iced tea -- that's a mistake you only make once.

## Leftover Ideas
- Pour into ice cube trays so your iced coffee never gets watered down
- Use as a base for coffee smoothies with banana and protein powder
- Mix with sweetened condensed milk for Vietnamese-style iced coffee
- Add to brownie or chocolate cake batter for a mocha twist
