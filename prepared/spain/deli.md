# Spain Deli & Prepared Foods

Costco Spain's deli is where the bulk-buying impulse meets the tapas instinct, and the results are genuinely great. The deli case leans heavily into Spanish classics — jamón, croquetas, tortilla, paella kits — alongside the usual Costco standards. If you've ever wished you could serve an entire tapas spread without actually cooking, this is your section.

## Spain-Specific Items

| Item | Price (€) | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Jamón Ibérico (Sliced) | €24.99 | Premium Spanish cured ham, thinly sliced and vacuum-packed. This is the good stuff — acorn-fed, nutty, melt-on-your-tongue jamón. At €24.99 for a generous pack, it's still a fraction of what you'd pay at a specialist shop. |
| Paella Kit | €14.99 | Seafood or mixed paella — feeds 4–6 people, ready to cook or just heat. Comes with rice, stock, protein, and sofrito. Not as good as your tía's, but your tía isn't feeding eight people on a Tuesday night. |
| Tortilla Española (Large) | €8.99 | Family-sized Spanish potato omelet — thick, golden, and served at room temperature. Feeds a crowd at a picnic or lasts a single person three glorious days. |
| Croquetas de Jamón | €7.99 | 20+ golden croquettes stuffed with silky jamón béchamel. Freeze half, fry as needed, thank yourself later. The crust-to-cream ratio is the whole game here, and these get it right. |
| Empanada Gallega | €9.99 | Large Galician-style pie — golden pastry over tuna or meat filling. Slices neatly, travels well, feeds a table. One of the best deli values in the case. |
| Patatas Bravas Kit | €6.99 | Crispy fried potatoes with two sauces: salsa brava (spicy tomato) and alioli. The patatas come pre-cut and par-fried — you just finish them in the oven or fryer. The brava sauce has actual kick, which not every restaurant version can claim. |
| Gazpacho (Family) | €5.99 | A big container of cold tomato soup — blended smooth with cucumber, pepper, garlic, and good olive oil. Summer essential in every Spanish household. Appears in the deli case around May and stays through September. |
| Salmorejo | €5.99 | Gazpacho's thicker, creamier cousin from Córdoba — tomato and bread blended into velvet, topped with jamón bits and hard-boiled egg. Summer seasonal alongside gazpacho. |
| Manchego Cheese | €9.99 | Spanish sheep's milk cheese in several ages — semicurado (3–6 months) for mild and creamy, curado (6–12 months) for sharper and firmer. Pairs with everything, especially membrillo (quince paste). |
| Chorizo | €7.99 | Spanish cured sausage — smoky, paprika-rich, and sliceable. Not the Mexican kind (different animal entirely). Works on a board, in a sandwich, or honestly just standing at the kitchen counter. |

### Summer Seasonal (May–September)
- **Gazpacho** and **salmorejo** in family-size containers — the deli case practically turns into a cold soup bar
- **Ensaladilla rusa** — Spanish-style potato salad with tuna, peas, and mayo. A summer tapa essential.
- Lighter paella kits emphasizing seafood over meat

### Christmas Seasonal (November–January)
- **Turrón** — Spanish nougat in two classic styles: turrón de Alicante (hard, with whole almonds) and turrón de Jijona (soft, ground almonds, almost fudge-like). Sold in bulk slabs, because one bar is never enough.
- **Polvorones** — crumbly, powdery almond shortbread cookies from Andalucía. They disintegrate on contact with your tongue, which is the point. Available in boxes of many more than you need, which is also the point.
- **Mantecados** — similar to polvorones but slightly denser and available in various flavors (cinnamon, lemon, chocolate). The line between polvorón and mantecado is a matter of regional pride and mild argument.
- **Roscón de Reyes ingredients** — see bakery section for the full roscón, but the deli carries marzipan, candied fruit, and cream for DIY versions.
