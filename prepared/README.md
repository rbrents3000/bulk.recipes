# Costco Prepared Foods by Country

> The stuff Costco already made so you don't have to. Food court, deli, bakery — across all 14 countries.

Costco doesn't just sell groceries. They run massive kitchens in every warehouse, and what comes out of them is surprisingly good, surprisingly cheap, and surprisingly different depending on where in the world you are. The $1.50 hot dog is universal, but everything around it changes.

## Fun Facts

- **Hot dogs:** Pork-based in Japan, Taiwan, China, and Australia. All-beef in the US. Everyone still pairs it with a soda.
- **Chicken bake replacements:** Bulgogi bake (Japan, Taiwan), pork bake (Korea), seafood bake (Taiwan), Mexican chicken bake (Sweden). The concept travels, the fillings adapt.
- **Gelato** replaces soft serve in Iceland, Sweden, and sometimes the UK. Honestly an upgrade.
- **Bubble tea** shows up in Korea, Taiwan, China, and Australia. The US food court is missing out.
- **Pizza** gets customized everywhere: bulgogi (Korea), truffle/Peking duck (China), seafood (Japan/Taiwan), al pastor (Mexico).
- The **$1.50 hot dog combo** maintains roughly the same price point worldwide. Some things are sacred.
