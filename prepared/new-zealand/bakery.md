# New Zealand Bakery

New Zealand's bakery culture runs on **meat pies, lamingtons, and the eternal pavlova debate** with Australia. Costco's Westgate bakery embraces all of it — in bulk, obviously.

The lamingtons move fast, the Afghan biscuits confuse international visitors, and the pavlova sits in the case like a meringue-based declaration of sovereignty.

## Seasonal

### Summer / Christmas (December–February)

- **Pavlova** gets extra shelf space — it *is* Christmas in New Zealand. Pre-decorated with cream, kiwifruit, and strawberries.
- **Fresh berry tarts** appear alongside the pav — summer berries are in season.
- **Christmas cakes and mince pies** — traditional fruitcake, dense with dried fruit and brandy, sized for a table of twelve.

### Winter (June–August)

- **Meat pies** dominate the case — cold, wet Auckland winters demand hot pastry.
- **Apple crumble cakes** — warm spices, stewed apple, crumble topping. Proper winter comfort.
- **Cheese scones** — savory, golden, best eaten warm with butter. A Kiwi morning tea staple.

## The Pavlova Question

Australia says they invented the pavlova. New Zealand says they invented the pavlova. The historical evidence is genuinely murky.

But Costco Westgate has clearly chosen a side — the party-sized pav is **front and center, year-round**, decorated with kiwifruit (the fruit, not the people). It's the bakery's bestseller in December and a strong seller the rest of the year.

> **The numbers:** Kiwis drove from as far as Hamilton — 90 minutes each way — to shop at Westgate on opening weekend. Some of them came back specifically for the lamingtons. Auckland traffic hasn't been the same since.
