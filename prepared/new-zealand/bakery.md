# New Zealand Bakery

New Zealand's bakery culture runs on meat pies, lamingtons, and the eternal pavlova debate with Australia. Costco's Westgate bakery embraces all of it — in bulk, obviously. The lamingtons move fast, the Afghan biscuits confuse international visitors, and the pavlova sits in the case like a meringue-based declaration of sovereignty.

## Seasonal

### Summer / Christmas (December–February)

- **Pavlova** gets extra shelf space — it *is* Christmas in New Zealand. The bakery version comes pre-decorated with cream, kiwifruit, and strawberries. Buy it whole and pretend you made it. Nobody will believe you, but nobody will care.
- **Fresh berry tarts** appear alongside the pav — summer berries are in season and the bakery takes full advantage.
- **Christmas cakes and mince pies** round out the holiday offerings. Traditional fruitcake, dense with dried fruit and brandy, in a size that could anchor a Christmas table for twelve.

### Winter (June–August)

- **Meat pies** dominate the case. Cold, wet Auckland winters demand hot pastry, and the 6-pack becomes a weeknight essential.
- **Apple crumble cakes** show up — warm spices, stewed apple, crumble topping. Proper winter comfort.
- **Cheese scones** — Savory, golden, and best eaten warm with a slab of butter. A Kiwi morning tea staple.
- Hearty baked goods in general take over. The bakery leans into what Kiwis want when the rain sets in: warm, filling, and ideally wrapped in pastry.

## The Pavlova Question

Australia says they invented the pavlova. New Zealand says they invented the pavlova. The historical evidence is genuinely murky, but the Costco Westgate bakery has clearly chosen a side — the party-sized pav is front and center, year-round, decorated with kiwifruit (the fruit, not the people). It's the bakery's bestseller in December and a strong seller the rest of the year. Some arguments are settled not by historians but by sales volume.

> **The numbers:** Kiwis drove from as far as Hamilton — 90 minutes each way — to shop at Westgate on opening weekend. Some of them came back specifically for the lamingtons. Auckland traffic hasn't been the same since.
