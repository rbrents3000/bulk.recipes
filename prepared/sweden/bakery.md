# Sweden Bakery

Sweden has a cinnamon bun culture so deep they invented a national holiday for it — **October 4th**, *Kanelbullens Dag*. *Fika* (the sacred coffee-and-pastry break) is practically a constitutional right.

Costco's bakery at Arninge walks into this arena with bulk-sized confidence. The **prinsesstårta** is the showstopper, but the **kanelbullar** sell faster than anything else in the case.

## Seasonal

### Semla Season (February–March)

- **Semla** — *The* bun. Cardamom dough filled with almond paste and whipped cream. Swedes eat semlor every Tuesday from Shrove Tuesday through Easter, especially on *Fettisdagen* (Fat Tuesday). Costco's version is generous with the cream.

> **Historical note:** King Adolf Frederick of Sweden ate himself to death on semlor in 1771. Fourteen, in one sitting. Costco's 6-pack format is practically a public health measure.

### Lucia (December 13)

- **Lussekatter** — Golden, S-shaped saffron buns with raisin eyes. Essential for Lucia celebrations, when children in white robes and candle crowns process through schools, churches, and living rooms across the country. The saffron is not optional.
- Stock goes fast — Lucia is December 13, but lussekatter start selling out the week before. Early shoppers win.

### Christmas (November–December)

- **Pepparkakor** — Thin, crispy ginger snaps consumed in industrial quantities from November through New Year's. Every Swedish household has a tin. Costco sells them in a tin the size of a small suitcase.
- **Saffransstjärnor** (saffron stars) — Festive saffron cookies that appear alongside the pepparkakor. Star-shaped, golden, and fragrant.

## Kanelbullens Dag (October 4)

On Cinnamon Bun Day, Swedish bakeries go into overdrive. Costco reportedly sells more kanelbullar on October 4th than any other day of the year. The 12-pack is the move — **buy two**, because you'll eat through the first one before you get home.

> **The rivalry:** Sweden now has two bulk-shopping empires — IKEA and Costco — and both sell baked goods. The kanelbulle comparison is inevitable, heated, and ongoing. Costco's version uses more cardamom. IKEA's is available in the furniture store. Choose your fighter.
