# China Bakery

China has 7 Costco warehouses (and growing), and the bakery section already reads like it was designed by someone who actually lives here. Egg tarts, red bean bread, pork floss bread, mango mille crepe — this isn't a token international selection, it's a legitimate Chinese bakery that happens to be inside a warehouse. The mooncakes during Mid-Autumn Festival are an event unto themselves. The line is legendary. Plan accordingly.

## Fall Seasonal (Sept-Oct)

### Mid-Autumn Festival Mooncakes

During Mid-Autumn Festival (usually September or October), the mooncake display takes over a significant chunk of the bakery. The line gets long. Legendarily long. "Take a number and go do your shopping" long. People buy multiple boxes for gifting — it's a whole cultural thing where the box matters almost as much as the mooncake inside.

- **In-House Mooncakes** — Costco bakes their own at a price point that undercuts specialty bakeries by 30-40%. Fillings rotate but typically include lotus seed paste with salted egg yolk (the classic), red bean, and mixed nuts (wu ren).
- **Premium Gift Boxes** — Beautifully packaged sets from well-known brands. These are the ones you bring to your boss or your in-laws. Presentation game is strong.
- **Pork Mooncakes (Su Shi)** — Flaky, layered pastry shell with savory pork filling. A Shanghai specialty that's become a Costco China cult item. These sell out first.

Pomelo also appears prominently during this season — it's the traditional Mid-Autumn fruit, and you'll see it stacked high near the bakery entrance.

## Winter Seasonal (Dec-Feb)

### Chinese New Year

The bakery shifts into full festive mode for Spring Festival:

- **Nian Gao (Rice Cakes)** — Sweet, sticky rice cakes that symbolize rising prosperity (the name sounds like "higher year" in Chinese). Sold plain or with red bean and dates. Slice and pan-fry them — trust us.
- **Festive Cake Gift Boxes** — Layer cakes and pastries in red-and-gold packaging, ready for gifting during the New Year visiting rounds.
- **Sesame Balls (Jian Dui)** — Deep-fried glutinous rice balls coated in sesame seeds, filled with sweet red bean or lotus paste. Crunchy outside, chewy inside.
- **Cranberry Nougat** — Soft, chewy nougat studded with cranberries and almonds, wrapped individually. A modern New Year snack that's become a staple.

### Winter Desserts

- **Chestnut Cake** — Rich, earthy chestnut cream layered cake. Chestnuts are a beloved winter street food in China, and this brings that flavor into cake form.
- **Strawberry Items** — Chinese strawberry season overlaps with winter, so strawberry tarts and cream cakes make an appearance in January and February.

## Also Available

Muffins, cinnamon rolls, and other US bakery items are stocked here too. The durian puff pastries, however, remain the real personality test of the bakery section — the aroma alone will tell you if the batch is fresh before you even round the corner.
